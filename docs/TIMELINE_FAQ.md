# FAQ

Answers to questions and forum posts. Sourced from external authors, publications, and research — not from any single timeline or project.

**Format rules:**
- Answers are brief, clinical, and in plain language.
- Short questions get short answers; long questions get long answers.
- Each answer ends with a compact **Sources** line citing external authors, books, or publications.
- Describe all argument sides and where the deadlock lies specifically — avoid one-sided advocacy.

## Q: Pyramids — tombs vs electromagnetic structures; mainstream rebuttal (texts, looting, ramps, copper tools, no geopolymer); can either side convince the other?

**Mainstream side:** Assumes Scaligerian chronology, gradual development, and institutional authority. Pyramids were tombs; Egyptian texts and workman graffiti say so. Looting and evolving funerary practice explain missing mummies and early lack of inscriptions. Ramps exist at Giza and elsewhere; copper + abrasive can cut stone; geopolymer and Davidovits are flawed. Rejects steel tools in masonry and alternative readings of Herodotus.

**Alternative side:** Assumes mass redaction, lost technology, and falsified chronology. Saturnian cosmology (Talbott, Cook) and catastrophist sources describe pyramids as electromagnetic or cathodic-protection structures at ley-line intersections. Material facts: no mummy in the Great Pyramid; Al-Ma'mun reportedly found gold coins, not a body; King's Chamber sarcophagus uninscribed. Fomenko and Davidovits: blocks are geopolymeric concrete; steel tools in masonry; Herodotus describes moulds, not ramps.

**Deadlock:** No shared criterion for what would settle the dispute. Mainstream won't run tests that might validate geopolymer; alternative side won't accept that copper+abrasive experiments or ramp finds could suffice. Each interprets the same facts through incompatible priors — mass redaction vs gradual development, lost tech vs known techniques. Authority (who gets to decide) is the real battleground. Movement would require independent replication or novel excavation both sides feel compelled to engage — unlikely given mutual dismissal.

**Sources:** Fomenko (*History: Fiction or Science?*, chronologia.org); Davidovits (*The Pyramids: An Enigma Solved*, geopolymer.org); Talbott (*The Saturn Myth*); Cook (Jno Cook, Saturnian chronology); Al-Ma'mun account (9th c.)
