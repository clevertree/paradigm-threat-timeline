# Tchaikovsky releases 1812 Overture

Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky composed the [1812 Overture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1812_Overture) in 1880, commissioned for the consecration of the Cathedral of Christ the Saviour in Moscow. Its premiere featured live cannon fire, church bells, and massed military bands.

The overture opens with **the Russian Orthodox hymn** *God Preserve Thy People*, drawn from the ancient Kievan liturgical tradition — the music of the old Hordian spiritual world. This is pitted against the *Marseillaise* (French Revolutionary anthem) and overwhelmed by cannon blasts and bells. The thematic contest is between the old Orthodox-Imperial order and the new Financial-Republican order Napoleon carried across Europe.
