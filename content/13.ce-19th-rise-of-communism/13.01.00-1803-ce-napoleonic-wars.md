# Napoleonic Wars

The Napoleonic Wars (1803–1815) were the final major armed conflict of the post-Hordian transition — the last attempt to create a unified continental European power resisting the City of London's financial empire. Napoleon's campaigns swept across old Imperial territories, dismantling feudal aristocracy and replacing it with the Napoleonic Code — rationalized law based on property rights, secular governance, and civil equality.

## Napoleon and the Financial Revolution

Napoleon's relationship to Europe's financial restructuring was paradoxical. He abolished the [Inquisition in Venice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inquisition) in 1806, liberated Jewish populations, and promoted their legal equality. Historian Patrice Gueniffey notes:

> *"He was, I think, ultimately rather good [for the Jews], because his policies for the Jewish community in France and the Empire promoted their assimilation into the French nation. The French revolution liberated the Jews but did not assimilate them. Napoleon took up the case again and decided to do for the Jews what he had done for other religions."*

Yet Napoleon's central bank project competed directly with the Bank of England's debt-based system. His [Continental System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_System) — an economic blockade of British goods — aimed to destroy the City of London's financial dominance. This is the real reason Britain never stopped fighting him. The Napoleonic Wars were a war between two financial systems: property-based order versus debt-based empire.

## The Revolutionary Prelude

The French Revolutionary Wars (1792–1797) were the military phase of France's transition from monarchical to financial governance:

- **1790**: France bans usury; Church taxes canceled; Church property nationalized
- **1792**: First Coalition forms (Austria, Prussia, Britain, Spain)
- **1793**: Royalist invasion crushed at Toulon; Napoleon gains fame
- **1799**: Napoleon seizes power in the [Coup of 18 Brumaire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/18_Brumaire)
- **1804**: Napoleon becomes Emperor

The Jacobins represented the same financial-revolutionary class that had transformed England. But France had a military genius who reorganized the chaos into genuine state power — rather than simply replacing aristocratic with financial governance.

## The Continental and Anti-Continental Coalitions

France faced **seven successive coalitions** organized by Britain and financed by the City of London. Each was broken militarily by Napoleon; each was reassembled through new British subsidies. The pattern reveals the asymmetry: Napoleon could win every battle and still lose, because his enemy's primary weapon was credit, not armies.

The Romanov-British partnership is critical context: the dynasty that partitioned Moscow Tartary now served as the eastern arm of British containment. The 1812 Russian campaign resulted not from military genius but deliberate scorched-earth strategy combined with climate — and the impossibility of occupying Moscow without triggering full Romanov-British resource mobilization.

## The Aftermath: Restored Order and Managed States

The [Congress of Vienna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna) (1814–1815) restored European monarchies with one crucial difference: every restored monarchy now operated within a framework of national debt to international banking houses (principally the Rothschilds, who had funded multiple sides and emerged as dominant European financial power). Political independence was real; financial independence was not. The post-Napoleonic order was the Westphalian system completed: sovereign states whose sovereignty operated within boundaries set by their creditors.
