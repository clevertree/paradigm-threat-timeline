# Orphan Trains, the Trail of Tears, and the Exile to Siberia

The [MudFlood Energetic Event](/timeline/evt-the-mudflood-and-world-cataclysm) (~1774) created a **population vacuum** — and the 19th century's characteristic institutions filled it. Orphan trains (200,000–250,000 children, 1854–1929), Indian boarding schools (408 schools, 1819–1969), the Trail of Tears, and Siberian exile are components of one system: **break lineage, erase memory, repopulate with compliant subjects.**

**Fomenko anchor:** Before Pugachev (1774), the Romanovs could not exile to Siberia — it was Moscow Tartary. Tobolsk became exile destination in 1790 (Radishev). 1830–50 Trail of Tears = same displacement pattern applied to the western Hordian remnant. Siberia = wreckage of the Rus-Horde Empire, the planet's new place of exile.

**The pattern:** Children as victims and instruments. Goal: sever generational connection so no inherited memory survives. Hordian world = lineage, oral tradition; post-MFEE order = institutions, documents, managed narrative.
