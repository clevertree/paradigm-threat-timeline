# Orphan Trains, the Trail of Tears, and the Exile to Siberia

The MudFlood Energetic Event (MFEE) of ~1774 did not merely destroy cities and petrify giants. It created a **population vacuum** — and the 19th century's most characteristic institutions were designed to fill it. Orphan trains, Indian boarding schools, the Trail of Tears, and Siberian exile are not separate stories. They are components of a single post-MFEE population-control system: **break lineage, erase memory, repopulate with compliant subjects.**

## The Orphan Phenomenon Predates the Trains

The first U.S. orphanage was established in 1729 by Ursuline nuns in New Orleans, caring for children orphaned by the Natchez uprising at Fort Rosalie. By the 1850s, an estimated **30,000 homeless children** lived on New York's streets. The Children's Aid Society (founded 1853) and the New York Foundling Hospital transported **200,000–250,000 children** from Eastern cities to rural Midwest foster homes between 1854 and 1929.

The standard explanation — immigration, Irish famine, financial panics — accounts for surface causes. The deeper question: why were so many children parentless? If the MFEE disrupted global populations in the 1770s–1790s, the orphan crisis of the mid-19th century is a **secondary cascade** — the grandchildren of catastrophe, raised in cities repopulated by survivors who had no lineage memory. The trains were the *institutionalization* of what was already underway.

## The Trail of Tears: Scope and Destination

The mainstream Trail of Tears narrative covers the forced relocation of ~60,000–100,000 Native Americans (Cherokee, Muscogee, Seminole, Chickasaw, Choctaw) between 1830 and 1850. The physical trail stretched ~5,045 miles across nine states. Deaths estimated at 13,200–16,700.

But the U.S. government also operated or supported **408 Indian boarding schools** across 37 states between 1819 and 1969. The Carlisle model — "Kill the Indian, save the man" — was explicit: cut hair, forbid native languages, replace tribal names with English, impose Christianity. Children were forcibly separated from families. Physical, emotional, and sexual abuse are documented in the 2022 Federal Indian Boarding School Initiative report.

### Open Investigation: Trail to Siberia via the Bering Strait

The Trail of Tears may have been longer and larger than historians present. The strongest and most resistant indigenous populations — those who would not submit to reservation life — may have been driven further: across the Bering Strait into Siberia, which had just been opened as a destination for exile. The weaker, more compliant remained on reservations where they could be managed. No primary-source evidence for a Bering–Siberia extension has been found. But the timing is suggestive, and the Bering Strait's post-MFEE geography may have still permitted crossing.

> *"Everything is clear. Until the end of the XVIII century the Romanovs could not exile anyone to Siberia simply because SIBERIA DID NOT BELONG TO THEM YET. The sequence is precise:
1. **1774–1775:** Pugachev defeated. Moscow Tartary falls.

Siberia — the wreckage of the Rus-Horde Empire — became the planet's new place of exile.

## Fomenko's "Wreckage" and the MFEE

Fomenko describes the Pugachev rebellion's aftermath as devastating, using the word **"wreckage"** — a term the existing paradigm-threat.net mudflood article highlights. But the scale of destruction Fomenko documents is far beyond what 18th-century conventional warfare could produce:

- All Hordian cities of Siberia reduced to wasteland
- Pre-rebellion maps show a flourishing kingdom with cities, rivers, and natural boundaries; post-rebellion maps show uninhabitable terrain
- Tobolsk — former Tartarian capital (Biblical "Tubal") — reduced to a prison camp
- Old maps show no Himalayas, no Grand Canyon, no Colorado River — landforms that appear only *after* the rebellion period

Fomenko attributes this to conventional warfare (Suvorov's campaign). But Suvorov's cavalry and artillery, however brilliant, could not melt cities, carve canyons, or bury continents in mud. The destruction Fomenko documents is more consistent with the [MFEE thesis](/timeline/evt-the-mudflood-and-world-cataclysm) — directed energy weapons fired from Mars during the rebellion, the secondary discharge devastating both planets.

**Fomenko may be describing MFEE damage without having the electrical framework to recognize it.** His political chronology is precise: the dates, the actors, the secret panels at Kazan and Orenburg that misrepresented the war's history. What he lacks is the physical mechanism. The "wreckage" he documents — the total destruction of a continental empire — is the political consequence of what was, in physical terms, a planetary-scale electrical event.

## The Pattern: Children as Weapons of Transformation

The post-MFEE displacement system used children as both victims and instruments:

- **Orphan trains (1854–1929):** 250,000 children redistributed to repopulate emptied territories
- **Indian boarding schools (1819–1969):** Break lineage, erase language, remake identity
- **Siberian exile (1790+):** Remove resisters from their communities permanently
- **Khmer Rouge (1975–1979):** Children as guards, infants bludgeoned to prevent revenge — the same template at industrial scale

The goal is always the same: **sever the connection between generations so no inherited memory survives.** The Hordian world governed through lineage, communal memory, and oral tradition. The post-MFEE order governs through institutions, documents, and managed narrative. To transition from one to the other requires destroying the carriers of the old memory — and the most efficient carriers are children who have been taught by their parents.
