# 1840 CE — Ecliptic pathway of the Absu last seen.

![Last Absu seen — zodiacal ecliptic pathway, AD 1840](/media/12.ce-18th-mudflood-and-pugachev/last-absu-seen.png)

The [Absu](https://saturniancosmology.org/cos.php) was Saturn's primordial plasma sheath — equatorial ring-layers enveloping Earth and the inner planets during the Golden Age. After the polar configuration collapsed in 2349 BC (Noah's Flood), the inner Absu fell — but an outer ring persisted.

## The Last Ring: The Uoroboros

One outer ring survived 2349 BC. Red, sitting ~6,000 miles above Earth's equator, Earth's shadow made it appear each night as a glowing snake whose head rose in the east and folded back on its own tail — the *Uoroboros*. The Egyptians called it *Apep*, Greeks *Apophis*, Hindus *Vritra*, Babylonians *Tiamat*, Australians the *Rainbow Serpent*. Same red equatorial ring, observed from every continent.

The ecliptic crossed this ring at equinoxes, forming a giant "X" — the Maya *Saint Andrew's Cross* hieroglyph. By **AD 600–800** the ring faded; by **~AD 900** it disappeared entirely.

## Absu Dust: 4,000 Years of Falling Particles

Even after the rings dissolved, remnant charged particles continued raining down for millennia. Charles Raspil (*"Spatters and Planetary Iconography,"* The Velikovskian, 1994) documented characteristic *spatter* patterns in artwork from the mid-second millennium BC through the **18th century AD** — Rhodian plates, Near Eastern reliefs, Egyptian papyri, Mesoamerican codices, Renaissance paintings. These appear as background texture, not symbols — objects actually seen.

## The Ecliptic Pathway: Last Seen AD 1840

The **ecliptic pathway** — zodiacal material strewn along the orbital plane by Jupiter's plasma bolts in the asteroid belt — remained faintly visible for another century.

> *"The destruction wrought by Jupiter with plasma bolts in the asteroid belt would have left dust and debris strewn along the ecliptic, causing the 'path of the Gods' to be marked like a highway. The lighted zodiac was last seen from Earth in AD 1840."*
> — Jno Cook, [SaturnianCosmology.org](https://saturniancosmology.org/flood.php#flood-g34)

- ~**AD 900**: Uoroboros fully gone

- **AD 1840**: Ecliptic zodiacal pathway last observed
