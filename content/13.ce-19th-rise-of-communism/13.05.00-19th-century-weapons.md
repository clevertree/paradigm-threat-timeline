## 1865: The Columbiad and the End of the Civil War

In 1865, with the American Civil War ending, Jules Verne published *From the Earth to the Moon* — a novel whose central device is a 900-foot [Columbiad cannon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbiad) firing a 30,000-pound aluminum projectile at 12,000 yards per second by means of 400,000 pounds of gun-cotton. The novel's **Gun Club of Baltimore** comprises Civil War artillerists who have perfected weapons of "colossal proportions" whose projectiles "cut in two some unoffending pedestrians."

Verne describes in precise engineering detail the arms race between penetrating projectiles and armor plate — Captain Nicholl builds successively thicker armor; J.T. Maston's Gun Club defeats each with heavier projectiles. The timing is not coincidental. The Civil War ended in April 1865. Verne's novel, serialized that same year, is set in the immediate aftermath. The Gun Club members are restless warriors whose weapons expertise exceeds anything officially acknowledged. **Verne was not predicting depleted uranium. He was describing a weapon system that already existed** — published as fiction, simultaneously disclosed and concealed, in the established pattern of [predictive programming](/timeline/evt-20th-century-predictive-programming-literature).
