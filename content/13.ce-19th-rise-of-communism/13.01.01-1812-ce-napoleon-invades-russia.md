# Napoleon invades Russia — Reverse Crusade I

Napoleon's invasion of Russia in 1812 — the Grande Armée's catastrophic six-month campaign across former Moscow Tartary — is **Reverse Crusade I**: the first organized pan-European invasion of Russia. Per Fomenko, the original Crusades were launched *by* the Rus-Horde against the west; 1812 reversed the direction. Neither Napoleon nor Hitler controlled their campaigns; both were directed by the same financial network (City of London) whose target was always Russia — the last successor of the Horde.

Borodino (7 Sept 1812), Moscow burned by its defenders, the winter retreat. The Russian army pursued Napoleon to Paris.
