## What Maxwell's Equations Actually Say

Maxwell discovered that dielectricity and magnetism are **conjugate** — two aspects of one phenomenon, propagating together at the speed of light. The speed of light itself is not an arbitrary constant. It derives directly from two **measured physical properties of the vacuum**:

$$c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0 \varepsilon_0}}$$

where $\varepsilon_0$ is the **permittivity** and $\mu_0$ is the **permeability** of free space.

These are properties. Properties of *what?* If space is empty, they are properties of nothing — which is a logical impossibility. Nothingness cannot have measurable characteristics. This equation is structurally identical to the speed of sound through air: the medium's properties determine the wave's velocity. Maxwell knew this. His equation says it plainly. (**Observation** — not a theory.)

## The Heaviside Reduction: What Was Lost

Maxwell's original system comprised **20 equations in 20 unknowns**, expressed in quaternion notation. Heaviside — an aether proponent himself — simplified this to the 4 vector equations taught today. The stripped terms described:

- **Scalar (longitudinal) electromagnetic waves** — waves that propagate along the axis, not transverse to it
- **Gravitational-electromagnetic coupling** — terms linking gravity to electromagnetism
- **The medium's internal stress tensor** — the aether's own mechanical properties

Modern physics says "Maxwell's equations don't require an aether." But the equations saying that are **Heaviside's reduction**, not Maxwell's original. The parts that required the aether were removed first, then the remainder was declared aether-free. (**Historical fact** — the original quaternion formulation is published and verifiable.)
