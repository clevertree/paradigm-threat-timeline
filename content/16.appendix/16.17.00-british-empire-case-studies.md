# British Empire as Deep State: Case Studies (Extended)

This appendix extends [The British Empire as Deep State Instrument](/timeline/evt-british-empire-as-deep-state-instrument). Detailed case studies.

---

**India and Pakistan (1947):** Two centuries deepening the Hindu-Muslim divide produced [Partition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_India) — 15 million displaced, up to 2 million dead. Resulting states fought four wars; nuclear standoff; dependent on Western arms and finance.

**Israel (1917–1948):** [Balfour Declaration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balfour_Declaration) promised a Jewish homeland while Britain encouraged Jewish immigration *and* armed Arab resistance. 1948 withdrawal produced immediate war. Both sides Western-dependent. Israel's founding inseparable from [false flags](/timeline/evt-false-flags) — King David Hotel, Lavon Affair, controlled opposition.

**Africa (1880s–1960s):** [Berlin Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scramble_for_Africa) (1884) drew borders cutting across ethnic boundaries. Colonial administration hardened tribal divisions (Hutu-Tutsi). 1960s independence → civil wars, millions dead. The earlier transatlantic slave trade (16th–19th c.) and Arab/Barbary trades preceded and overlapped colonial rule; see [Slave Trades and the Horde Fracture](/timeline/evt-slave-trades-horde-fracture).

**Japan and China:** [Opium Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opium_Wars) broke Chinese sovereignty; [Meiji Restoration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meiji_Restoration) engagement created Japanese military power. Sino-Japanese rivalry keeps Asia's largest powers focused on each other.

**Hong Kong (1997):** Decades constructing incompatible institutions — independent judiciary, separate currency — produced permanent pressure point for Western leverage.
