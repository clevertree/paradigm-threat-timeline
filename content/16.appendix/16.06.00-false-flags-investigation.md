## I. Pearl Harbor: The Evidence of Advance Knowledge

### A. The McCollum Memo (October 7, 1940)

Lieutenant Commander Arthur H. McCollum, Office of Naval Intelligence, submitted a memorandum to Navy Captains Walter S. Anderson and Dudley Knox. The memo — classified until 1994 — outlined eight specific provocations designed to lead Japan into an overt act of war. The concluding line: *"If by these means Japan could be led to commit an overt act of war, so much the better."*

Full text declassified and published in Robert Stinnett, *Day of Deceit* (2000), pp. 275–277. Stinnett claims all eight action items were implemented. Critics note there is no paper trail proving Roosevelt personally saw the memo, but the circumstantial evidence is strong: Roosevelt's own policies mirror the eight points exactly.

**Source:** [McCollum Memo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McCollum_memo), National Archives.

### B. The Purple Code and Withheld Intelligence

The U.S. Army cryptographers broke Japan's highest-security diplomatic code ("Purple") in 1940. Decrypted intercepts — codenamed "Magic" — were delivered to senior officials including Roosevelt almost daily.

A 14-part message decoded on December 6–7, 1941, made plain Japan's intention to break off diplomatic relations. Roosevelt, upon reading just 13 parts, reportedly declared: *"This means war."* General Marshall ordered a warning sent to Pacific bases, but due to "atmospheric transmission conditions" the message was sent via Western Union rather than military radio — and arrived **after the attack was already underway**.

Key intercepts that were not forwarded to Hawaiian commanders:
- November 24, 1941: Admiral Yamamoto to strike force — order to advance into Hawaiian waters
- Station H, Hawaii, intercepted multiple messages indicating intentions of war in the days before December 7
- None were forwarded to Admiral Kimmel or General Short

**Sources:** Clausen & Lee, *Pearl Harbor: Final Judgement* (2001); Stinnett, *Day of Deceit*; [Pearl Harbor advance-knowledge conspiracy theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pearl_Harbor_advance-knowledge_conspiracy_theory).

### D. The Missing Carriers and Expendable Fleet

None of the three Pacific Fleet aircraft carriers were at Pearl Harbor during the attack:
- **USS Enterprise** — delivering fighters to Wake Island (delayed by weather; originally due back December 6)
- **USS Lexington** — delivering fighters to Midway Island
- **USS Saratoga** — in routine refit at Puget Sound

The ships that **were** in port consisted primarily of obsolete battleships. Admiral Richardson had been relieved of command specifically for protesting the relocation of the fleet to the vulnerable Hawaiian position. No court martial was ever held for Kimmel or Short — because a trial would have required disclosure of the broken codes and withheld intelligence.

**Source:** Stinnett, *Day of Deceit*; [ibiblio.org/pha/myths/Missing_Carriers](https://www.ibiblio.org/pha/myths/Missing_Carriers.html).

## II. The Nuclear Question

### A. Conventional Devastation vs. Atomic Claims

Before Hiroshima (August 6, 1945), sixty-six Japanese cities had been area-bombed with napalm:
- **Tokyo, March 10, 1945:** 279 B-29s dropped 1,665 tons of napalm. ~100,000 killed in a single night. 270,000 buildings destroyed
- Total conventional firebombing: **180 square miles** of 67 cities destroyed, 300,000+ killed, 400,000+ injured
- Hiroshima and Nagasaki were **deliberately left unbombed** prior to the atomic attacks to provide undamaged demonstration targets

The destruction attributed to the atomic bombs was therefore presented to a public already accustomed to massive city-level destruction from conventional bombing — but packaged with an entirely new narrative of **instantaneous vaporization** and **radiation that killed for miles**.

### B. The DU Hypothesis

The symptoms reported by Hiroshima and Nagasaki survivors — progressive wasting diseases, cancers developing over years, generational health effects — are more consistent with **depleted uranium contamination** than with acute radiation from a fission weapon:
- DU produces chronic, energy-draining illness through heavy metal toxicity and low-level alpha radiation
- Clean fission radiation at sub-lethal doses can produce [radiation hormesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radiation_hormesis) — paradoxical health benefits
- The pattern of illness in Japan more closely resembles DU exposure zones in Iraq and the Balkans than predicted fission-bomb radiation profiles

### C. The Tsar Bomba as Narrative Countermeasure

The Soviet Union detonated the AN602 hydrogen bomb ("Tsar Bomba") on October 30, 1961 — a claimed 50-megaton yield, the largest nuclear device ever tested. The manufactured footage showed a staggering mushroom cloud and reports claimed shockwaves were detected worldwide.

Whether the Tsar Bomba was real, exaggerated, or entirely fictional, its **narrative function** was clear: it trapped the West in its own nuclear mythology. Having insisted that fission bombs could incinerate cities in an instant, the Americans could not deny the Soviet claim of a fusion bomb orders of magnitude more powerful. The result was Mutually Assured Destruction — a **strategic equilibrium** that prevented either side from using nuclear threats for unilateral advantage.

The Russian "fusion bomb" was thus a **narrative antibody** against the Deep State's "fission bomb" fear campaign.

## III. The Battle of Los Angeles (February 24–25, 1942)

### Timeline of Events
- **February 23, 1942:** Japanese submarine I-17 shells Ellwood Oil Field near Santa Barbara — minimal damage but enormous psychological impact
- **February 24, 7:18 PM:** Alert called after ONI warning of expected attack within 10 hours; lifted at 10:23 PM
- **February 25, 2:25 AM:** Air raid sirens sound across Los Angeles County; total blackout ordered
- **3:16–4:14 AM:** 37th Coast Artillery Brigade fires .50-caliber machine guns and 12.8-pound anti-aircraft shells; over 1,400 rounds expended
- **7:21 AM:** All clear sounded

### Results
- No bombs dropped. No enemy aircraft downed. No wreckage recovered
- Five civilians died: three in car crashes, two of heart attacks
- Buildings and vehicles damaged by shell fragments

### Official Explanations
- Secretary Knox (Navy): "False alarm" due to war nerves
- General Marshall (Army): Possibly enemy agents using commercial airplanes for psychological warfare
- U.S. Office of Air Force History (1983): Weather balloons triggered the alarm
- Japan (post-war): Confirmed they flew no aircraft over Los Angeles

### The Anomaly
Whatever was in the sky over Los Angeles that night **survived 1,400+ anti-aircraft rounds without damage**, did not drop bombs, and was not Japanese. The official explanation — a weather balloon — is the same explanation offered for Roswell five years later. Contemporary photographs show searchlights converging on a luminous object. ## IV. The Encyclopedia of 20th-Century False Flags

### USS Maine (1898)
The explosion that sank the USS Maine in Havana Harbor — blamed on Spain, triggering the Spanish-American War — was almost certainly internal. Evidence suggests the blast blew **outward** from the hull, not inward as a mine would produce. Spain never claimed responsibility; Spanish officials actually helped rescue survivors. "Remember the Maine" was a media creation of William Randolph Hearst and Joseph Pulitzer.

### Reichstag Fire (1933)
The burning of the German parliament building was blamed on communist Marinus van der Lubbe. Hitler used it to suspend civil liberties via the [Reichstag Fire Decree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reichstag_Fire_Decree) — the template for every emergency-powers seizure since. The German population was subsequently forced into Nazism through stolen elections, propaganda, and terror — not genuine popular support.

### Manchurian Incident (1931)
The Japanese Kwantung Army detonated a bomb on the South Manchurian Railway and blamed it on Chinese forces — using the incident as pretext to occupy Manchuria and establish the puppet state of Manchukuo.

### Gulf of Tonkin (1964)
The alleged North Vietnamese attack on USS Maddox — used by LBJ to escalate the Vietnam War through the Gulf of Tonkin Resolution — was [later acknowledged to have been largely fabricated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Tonkin_incident). NSA documents declassified in 2005 confirmed the second attack never occurred.

### USS Liberty (1967)
Israel attacked the USS Liberty during the Six-Day War, killing 34 American sailors. Israel claimed mistaken identity despite the ship flying a large American flag and being clearly marked. Survivors maintain the attack was deliberate.

### Lavon Affair (1954)
Israeli agents planted bombs in Egyptian civilian targets — cinemas, post offices — disguised as Muslim Brotherhood operations. The operation was exposed when an agent was caught. Israel initially denied involvement; the truth was formally acknowledged only in 2005.

### Operation Gladio (1960s–1990s)
A NATO-backed network of stay-behind paramilitary operations conducted false flag terrorist attacks across Europe — particularly in Italy — to discredit left-wing movements. Bombings, assassinations, and kidnappings were carried out and blamed on communist groups.

### King David Hotel (1946)
The Irgun (led by future Israeli Prime Minister Menachem Begin) bombed the British Mandate headquarters in Jerusalem, killing 91 people. The attackers disguised themselves as Arab workers.

### Operation Northwoods (1962)
A proposed plan by the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff to stage terrorist attacks on American soil and blame them on Cuba — including sinking refugee boats, hijacking planes, and bombing American cities. The plan was rejected by President Kennedy. It was declassified in 1997.

### Oklahoma City (1995)
The bombing of the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building killed 168 people and was attributed to Timothy McVeigh. Multiple witnesses reported additional unexploded devices inside the building. Security camera footage was confiscated and never fully released.

### 1972 Munich Olympics
Eleven Israeli athletes were killed by the Palestinian group Black September. The security failures were comprehensive, the media coverage perfectly positioned, and the political consequences perfectly aligned with Israeli and Western intelligence objectives.
