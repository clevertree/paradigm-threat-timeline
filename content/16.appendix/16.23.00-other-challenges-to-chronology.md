## Where They Agree

1. **Something is missing** — archaeological, stratigraphic, astronomical, and statistical evidence all point to phantom centuries.
2. **Gap is medieval** — concentrated between late antiquity and High Middle Ages, when Church controlled scriptoria.
3. **Mechanism is institutional** — fabrication by institutions controlling record-keeping.

## Where They Diverge

**Size**: Estimates range from ~297 years (Illig) to 1,723 (this project). But they nest: Illig's 297 is subset of Fomenko's 1,053; Heinsohn targets 1st millennium BCE; Velikovsky limited to Egyptian chronology; this project extends Fomenko by adding ~670 years from Cook's orbital chronology.

**Motive**: Illig proposes one-time political fabrication. Heinsohn sees systemic inflation. Velikovsky saw scholarly error. Fomenko identifies institutional momentum. ## The Julian Calendar Proof

Julian calendar gains ~1 day every 128 years. By Gregory XIII's 1582 reform, 10 days accumulated. But 1,582 years since adoption (46 BCE) should produce ~12.4 days. The **~2.4 missing days** ≈ **~307 phantom years** — close to Illig's 297. Fomenko absorbs this into larger 1,053-year shift: calendar was not running because those years did not happen.
