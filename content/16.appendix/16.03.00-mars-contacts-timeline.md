## 1894: Strange Light on Mars — Lick & Nice Observatories

A "great light" was observed on the illuminated part of Mars during the opposition of 1894. Reported first at Lick Observatory, then confirmed by **Perrotin of Nice**. H.G. Wells opened his novel with this observation, interpreting it as *"the casting of the huge gun"* — the vast pit sunk into Mars from which the invasion cylinders were fired. Ten similar flashes followed over ten consecutive nights before the first cylinder arrived.

Whether Wells was fictionalising an astronomical anomaly or disclosing an intelligence briefing, the observation itself is documented in the astronomical record.

## 1897: "The Crystal Egg" — Two-Way Surveillance Disclosure

Wells published "The Crystal Egg" in *The New Review* (May 1897), months before serialising *War of the Worlds*. The story describes a paired crystal device enabling **real-time two-way surveillance** between Earth and Mars. Through the crystal, the observer sees: Martian architecture (circular openings, no doors, metallic tracery), winged Martians in flight, wingless large-headed Martians, white translucent bipeds (livestock?), and a **vast mechanism of shining metals** of extraordinary complexity.

~20 crystal mast stations were observed on terraced Martian platforms. After the Earth-side crystal's owner died, it was purchased by *"a tall, dark man in grey"* and disappeared — intelligence recovery.

This is not fiction structure. It is a **surveillance technology briefing** formatted as a short story.

## 1897–1898: War of the Worlds — The Invasion Account

*War of the Worlds* was serialised in *Pearson's Magazine* (April–December 1897), then published as a book by Heinemann in January 1898. The narrative provides operationally precise detail: cylinder dimensions (~30 yards diameter), Heat-Ray characteristics (microwave-range directed energy matching the modern Active Denial System), Black Smoke deployment (chemical weapon), tripod assembly sequences, Martian physiology (bodiless brains dependent on machine infrastructure), and strategic objectives (*"complete demoralisation and the destruction of any opposition"* — not extermination).

Two **unauthorised** American serialisations immediately relocated the invasion: the *New York Evening Journal* (December 1897–January 1898) moved it to **New York City**; the *Boston Post* (1898) set it in and near **Boston**. These were not creative adaptations — they were **field reports repackaged for American geography**, suggesting the original account was not purely English.

**Timing:** The novel appeared in **1898** — sixteen years before the outbreak of **World War I** (1914). This is the first instance of the pattern: *a War of the Worlds disclosure event precedes a world war.* The 1938 broadcast precedes WW2 by the same margin of proximity (see below). ## 1899: Tesla Detects Signals from Mars

During experiments at his Colorado Springs laboratory in 1899, Nikola Tesla observed unusual signals from his receiver which he speculated to be communications from another planet. He wrote in a December 1899 letter to a reporter and to the Red Cross Society in December 1900 that the signals appeared to be *"intelligently controlled."* In his February 1901 *Collier's Weekly* article "Talking With Planets," Tesla stated the signals could have come from Mars, Venus, or other planets.

The press jumped to "Mars." Tesla never retracted the claim. His Wardenclyffe Tower project (1901–1905), funded by J.P. Morgan with $150,000, was ostensibly for transatlantic wireless communication — but Tesla's stated ambition was **interplanetary communication**. Morgan pulled funding. The tower was demolished.

Tesla also developed a "teleforce" beam weapon (1934) whose principles — open-ended vacuum tube, gas jet seal, tungsten slugs charged to millions of volts and directed by electrostatic repulsion — bear structural resemblance to the Heat-Ray described by Wells. His DIA-adjacent contacts and the FBI/Office of Alien Property seizure of his papers within two days of his death (January 1943) suggest his work intersected classified Mars-related intelligence.

## 1901: New York Times Reports Light Flash from Mars

On January 16, 1901, the *New York Times* reported observations by **Professor Pickering** of Harvard, based on data from **Lowell Observatory**, of a luminous shaft of light emanating from Mars lasting **70 minutes**. The article treated the observation as astronomical curiosity. Lowell Observatory — Percival Lowell's institution — was then the world centre for Mars canal research and had been documenting what Lowell interpreted as an engineered canal network since 1895.

A 70-minute sustained light emission is not a natural atmospheric phenomenon. It is consistent with a directed energy projection or signalling event.

## 1942: Battle of Los Angeles — 1,400 Rounds at Unknown Targets

On the night of February 24–25, 1942, the U.S. military fired **over 1,400 anti-aircraft rounds** at unidentified targets over Los Angeles. The Office of Naval Intelligence had warned of an expected attack within 10 hours on February 24. Air raid sirens sounded at 2:25 AM. Firing continued from 3:16 to 4:14 AM. **Five civilians died** (three from car accidents during blackout, two from heart attacks attributed to stress).

Key anomalies:
- **No bombs were dropped.** No enemy aircraft were downed. No wreckage recovered.
- Secretary of the Navy Frank Knox called the entire event a **"false alarm"** the next day — contradicting the Army's insistence that real targets were present
- Japan confirmed post-war they flew **no aircraft over Los Angeles** that night
- The Air Force later attributed the incident to a **"weather balloon"** and war nerves — the same explanation used for Roswell five years later
- Contemporary photographs show searchlights converging on a luminous object

The incident occurred 10 weeks after Pearl Harbor, during genuine invasion anxiety. Whatever the military fired at for nearly an hour was not Japanese aircraft. The object(s) survived 1,400+ anti-aircraft rounds without damage.

## 1947: Roswell — "Flying Disc" Announced, Then Retracted

In early July 1947, debris was recovered from a ranch near **Corona, New Mexico.** On July 8, the **Roswell Army Air Field** issued a press release announcing the recovery of a "flying disc." The release was retracted within 24 hours and replaced with the claim that the debris was a **weather balloon.**

In 1994, the Air Force admitted the weather balloon story was a cover — the debris was actually from **Project Mogul**, a classified nuclear test detection balloon. But the original RAAF press release exists in the historical record: a military installation announced the recovery of a non-terrestrial craft, then immediately retracted it under pressure.

Key intelligence officer **Major Jesse Marcel**, who handled the debris, told journalist Stanton Friedman in 1978 that the material was unlike anything terrestrial — thin metallic material that could not be dented or burned, with strange symbols. Marcel maintained this account until his death.

## 1949: War of the Worlds Broadcast — Quito, Ecuador (Deadliest)

On February 12, 1949, Radio Quito broadcast a Spanish-language adaptation of *War of the Worlds* by **Eduardo Alcaraz**, formatted as breaking news interrupting regular programming. Unlike the 1938 broadcast, this one triggered **lethal violence**:

## 1984: CIA Remote Viewing of Mars — Stargate Project

In 1984, the CIA's **Stargate Project** (codenamed variously "Grill Flame," "Center Lane," "Sun Streak") conducted a remote viewing session targeting **Mars, approximately one million years BCE.** The declassified transcript records a monitor/subject dialogue describing:

- Volcanos and gas pockets
- Strange plants
- Pyramidal structures
- The interior of *"a larger boat"* with *"rounded walls and shiny metal"*
- Tall, thin beings in distress, aware their civilisation was ending

The Stargate Project operated from 1977 to 1995, cost $20 million, and was run out of Fort Meade, Maryland, by the Defense Intelligence Agency and SRI International. Key personnel included **Harold Puthoff**, **Russell Targ**, **Ingo Swann**, and **Joseph McMoneagle**. Ed Dames, a session monitor, specifically pushed CRV sessions targeting **Mars, UFOs, and Atlantis.**

The program was terminated in 1995 after a CIA-commissioned review claimed remote viewing *"had not been proved to work by a psychic mechanism"* and *"had never provided an adequate basis for actionable intelligence."* The declassified Mars session transcript was released as part of the CREST archive in January 2017.

Whether or not one accepts remote viewing as valid, the significant fact is: **the United States intelligence community allocated $20 million over 18 years to a program that repeatedly targeted Mars** — an allocation inconsistent with the "dead planet" official position.
