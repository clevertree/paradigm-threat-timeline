# The Thunderbolts Project: A Case Study in Managed Disclosure

This article provides the detailed evidentiary record supporting the assessment in [Controlled Opposition: Managed Disclosure and Gatekeeping](/timeline/evt-controlled-opposition). For the general pattern of controlled opposition, see that article.

## Overview

The Thunderbolts Project (thunderbolts.info), founded by David Talbott and Wallace Thornhill, is the most prominent organisation promoting Electric Universe theory and Saturnian cosmology to the public. Their work draws from Immanuel Velikovsky's *Worlds in Collision* (1950) and incorporates plasma physics. For many independent researchers, the Thunderbolts Project is the gateway into catastrophist and Saturnian studies.

It is also, in the assessment of multiple independent researchers including Jno Cook, a textbook case of managed disclosure — an operation that approaches forbidden knowledge closely enough to attract genuine seekers, while systematically **excluding the most significant findings**.

Critically, **Velikovsky himself never published his Saturn theory**. He died in 1979 with the work unfinished. The Thunderbolts group positioned itself as the continuation of Velikovsky's research — but Velikovsky's own correspondence shows he was already warning Talbott that the work was going off track.

## Velikovsky's Warning to Talbott (1975)

In a letter dated **January 29, 1975** — preserved in the Velikovsky Archive (varchive.org, correspondence index, 1975) — Immanuel Velikovsky wrote directly to David Talbott after reading his typescript:

> *"Your removal of the Sun from any role in mythology goes, in my view, a little too far. It is true that in late antiquity and especially since Macrobius almost all mythology was made to represent the Sun, a vogue very much in mode at the beginning of the 19th century (Goethe's time) but also later — see my references in the first chapter of Oedipus and Akhnaton; but if there was a Day Sun besides a Night Sun, it could not escape having some role in mythology."*

This is devastating in context. Velikovsky — the founding figure whose work the entire Thunderbolts project claims to continue — told Talbott directly that he was **removing essential elements from the cosmological picture**. Talbott's response was not to correct course but to proceed with exactly the exclusions Velikovsky warned against. The man whose unfinished work gave the project its legitimacy was being overridden while still alive.

This establishes that the controlled opposition function was present **from the very inception of the project** — not something that developed later.

## What Thunderbolts Gets Right

The Thunderbolts group correctly identifies that Saturn was once Earth's primary celestial body, that the "Polar Configuration" of planets existed, and that plasma physics rather than gravitational mechanics explains many features of the Solar System. Their documentaries (*Symbols of an Alien Sky*, the *Discourses on an Alien Sky* series) introduce mythological analysis and plasma cosmology to a broad audience. Wal Thornhill's contributions to electric field cosmology are genuine.

## What Thunderbolts Systematically Excludes

Jno Cook, author of *Recovering the Lost World: A Saturnian Cosmology* (2001–2026), attended multiple Electric Universe conferences (2013, 2014, 2016) and spent decades building what is arguably the most comprehensive Saturnian chronology in existence. His assessment of the Thunderbolts group is devastating:

**1. Denial of post-3147 BC catastrophism.** Talbott, Thornhill, and Cardona "hold that nothing ever happened after the so-called Polar Configuration came apart in 3147 BC: not with Venus in 1500 BC or with Mars after 800 BC." Cook calls this an "astounding oversight," noting that "the huge assembly of mythology which recounts these events [is] spread over three continents, with Mesoamerica detailing this with identifiable dates." The Thunderbolts timeline stops precisely where the most dangerous connections — to Biblical events, classical-era catastrophes, and the true age of civilisation — would need to begin.

**2. Planetary misidentifications.** Talbott identified the planet between Saturn and Mars in the polar configuration as Venus. Cook determines it was Mercury, not Venus: "David Talbott had held this as a condition of the polar stack, but had identified the planet between Saturn and Mars as Venus. He has also remained unaware of the planets above Saturn." Elsewhere: "as pointed out by Talbott (who misidentifies Mercury as Venus)."

**3. "Science fiction" models.** Cook describes the Thunderbolts "left behind" satellite scenario as "science fiction" with "too many unsupported 'iffy' clauses." He calls the "corkscrew single entry" concept for Saturn's arrival "pure nonsense," adding that plasma interactions "cannot retard the travel of a planet. It would take the force of an electric field interaction, which is **beyond the conceptual basis of the Thunderbolts group**."

**4. Systematic avoidance of Biblical sources.** "The Saturnian people (of Thunderbolts.info) have steadfastly avoided bringing the Bible into discussion. I find this a remarkable omission." For a group claiming to study ancient mythology and its cosmological origins, excluding the single most widely distributed ancient text — which contains extensive catastrophist material — is not an omission but a **policy**.

**5. Deletion of challenges.** When challenged on his definition of the "Warrior King" archetype upon the release of *Symbols of an Alien Sky*, Talbott's response was not debate but erasure. As documented by forum user "grey cloud" (August 7, 2010): *"You may recall I challenged Talbott on his definition of this 'archetype' when Symbols of an Alien Sky was released. His 'refutation' consisted of him deleting the entire thread."*

**6. Editorial gatekeeping at AEON Journal.** Researcher C. Leroy Ellenberger documented how Talbott, Ev Cochrane, and Dwardu Cardona controlled the AEON journal: in the first 16 issues, these three accounted for **34% of all articles, 44% of all article pages**, and 7 of the 9 longest articles. Outside contributions faced "unexplained deletions and unreasonable delays in publication." Ellenberger's invited contributions waited **two years** in the queue while George Talbott's attack piece against him was published immediately. When Ellenberger's memoir exceeded standard length, the editor "adamantly refused to relax the type format standards" — yet Talbott's own 47-page article received smaller type and wider margins specifically to fit.

**7. Cardona's methodology.** Criticism of Dwardu Cardona's published work noted: "Nowhere in the book is a single mythological tale used, let alone analysed. Instead the reader is presented with Cardona's opinion... supported by certain key words and phrases culled from everywhere and anywhere and arranged to suit Cardona's 'theory'." For a group that claims mythological analysis as its foundation, publishing works that contain no actual myth analysis is a revealing contradiction.
