## Trail of Tears: Scope and Detail

- **Forced relocation:** ~60,000–100,000 Native Americans (Cherokee, Muscogee, Seminole, Chickasaw, Choctaw) between 1830 and 1850
- **Physical trail:** ~5,045 miles across nine states
- **Deaths:** 13,200–16,700 estimated
- **Indian boarding schools:** 408 across 37 states (1819–1969). Carlisle model: "Kill the Indian, save the man" — cut hair, forbid native languages, replace tribal names, impose Christianity. 2022 Federal Indian Boarding School Initiative documented physical, emotional, sexual abuse.

## Open Investigation: Trail to Siberia via Bering Strait

The Trail of Tears may have been longer and larger than historians present. The strongest, most resistant populations may have been driven across the Bering Strait into Siberia, which had just opened as exile destination. Weaker, more compliant remained on reservations. No primary-source evidence found; timing is suggestive; post-MFEE Bering geography may have permitted crossing.

## Fomenko's "Wreckage" — MFEE Consistency

Fomenko describes Pugachev aftermath as devastating "wreckage." Scale of destruction exceeds conventional warfare:

- All Hordian cities of Siberia → wasteland
- Pre-rebellion maps: flourishing kingdom; post-rebellion: uninhabitable terrain
- Tobolsk (Biblical Tubal) → prison camp
- Old maps: no Himalayas, no Grand Canyon, no Colorado River — landforms appear only *after* rebellion period

Fomenko attributes to Suvorov's campaign. Suvorov's cavalry could not melt cities or carve canyons. Destruction consistent with [MFEE thesis](/timeline/evt-the-mudflood-and-world-cataclysm) — directed energy from Mars, secondary discharge devastating both planets. **Fomenko may be describing MFEE damage without the electrical framework.**
