## Trail of Tears: Scope and Detail

- **First reservation:** 1758 — Brotherton Indian Reservation, New Jersey (**before MFEE**)
- **Official forced relocation:** 1830–1839 — ~60,000–100,000 Native Americans (Cherokee, Muscogee, Seminole, Chickasaw, Choctaw)
- **Physical trail:** ~5,045 miles across nine states
- **Deaths:** 13,200–16,700 estimated (official; likely higher)
- **Indian Appropriations Act:** 1851 — institutionalizes federal reservation system
- **Indian boarding schools:** 408 across 37 states (1819–1969). Carlisle model: "Kill the Indian, save the man" — cut hair, forbid native languages, replace tribal names, impose Christianity. 2022 Federal Indian Boarding School Initiative documented physical, emotional, sexual abuse.

**Total duration of Trail of Tears campaign:** 93 years (1758–1851), arguably 211 years (1758–1969).

By 1839, Native Americans had become what we remember them as: **without a kingdom, without technology, using sticks and stones.** This was not how they began. **It is how they were made.**

## Open Investigation: Trail to Siberia via Bering Strait

The Trail of Tears may have been longer and larger than historians present. The strongest, most resistant populations may have been driven across the Bering Strait into Siberia, which had just opened as exile destination. Weaker, more compliant remained on reservations. No primary-source evidence found; timing is suggestive; post-MFEE Bering geography may have permitted crossing.
