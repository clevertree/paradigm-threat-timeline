# Soviet Historiography: Bogdanov and Suppressed Science (Extended)

This appendix extends [Soviet Historiography](/timeline/evt-soviet-historiography). The Bogdanov/Lactobacillus case.

---

Soviet falsification extended beyond history — the same apparatus suppressed **science** that contradicted institutional priorities. Beginning in the 1960s, Bulgarian physician **Dr. Ivan Bogdanov** discovered that cell fragments of *Lactobacillus bulgaricus* (yogurt bacterium isolated by Stamen Grigorov, 1905) could induce **cancer remission** in myeloma patients. The Soviet state absorbed this into a classified **State R&D program (1980)** at **Enzymes Ltd., Kiev**. Under Dr. Liubov Shynkarenko, researchers found the fragments also protected against **radiation damage and mutagenic activity** — findings with obvious value to a state running nuclear and biological weapons programs under **Biopreparat**.

Products: Lactoflor, Extrabiol, Liasten. USSR collapse dissolved the program; Liasten approved in Ukraine; Del-Immune V (NJ) descended from the research. Same pattern as history: absorbed, classified, lost. Fomenko marginalized; Bogdanov's breakthroughs fell between Soviet secrecy and Western pharma.
