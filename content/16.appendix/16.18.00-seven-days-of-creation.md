# Seven Days of Creation (Extended)

Every major monotheist tradition has inherited a seven-day creation account in which the universe springs into being over a single week. This article argues that the account is **literally true** — but compressed. Each "day" is a cosmological epoch lasting **hundreds of years**, bookended by a planetary climax: a god disappearing, a calendar changing, a new body dominating the sky. The compression from centuries to days was performed by later monotheist editors who retained the seven-beat structure while stripping the timescale entirely.

The internal contradiction that has never been satisfactorily resolved — **light and darkness exist on Days 1–3, but the Sun and Moon are not created until Day 4** — dissolves completely once you recognise that time was already being reckoned not by our Sun, but by **Kronos**.

---

## The Contradiction Built Into Genesis

Genesis 1 records:

- **Day 1:** God creates light; separates light from darkness; calls them Day and Night
- **Day 2:** Creates the firmament; separates waters above from waters below
- **Day 3:** Dry land appears; vegetation grows
- **Day 4:** *"Let there be lights in the firmament of the heaven to divide the day from the night"* — the Sun, Moon, and stars are made
- **Day 5:** Waters fill with life; birds appear
- **Day 6:** Land animals and humanity created
- **Day 7:** God rests

The contradiction is stark. "Evening and morning were the first day" — but there is **no Sun on Day 1**. Days and nights pass for three days before the body that creates days and nights is fabricated. Every theological tradition has attempted to explain this away:

- *"God's light was different from the Sun's light"* — asserted without mechanism
- *"The Sun was created on Day 1 but placed in its orbit on Day 4"* — contradicts the plain text
- *"Days are figurative aeons"* — true in the deepest sense, but still leaves the Sun-before-luminaries problem unsolved

The Saturnian model does not require apologetics. It provides the mechanism.

## Kronos: The Original Timekeeping Sun

Saturn was called [Kronos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cronus) by the Greeks. The word *kronos* (*χρόνος*) means **time**. The name was not a metaphor or honorific — it was a description of a **physical observation**.

As documented in [The 225-Day Year](/timeline/evt-the-225-day-year):

> *"We can actually claim that Saturn invented time — when it no longer blazed, but had reduced its output or dropped to dark mode. That is when a crescent would be seen at the edge of the globe, rotating counterclockwise on a daily basis."*
> — Jno Cook, *Saturnian Cosmology*, Chapter 14

Saturn's visible crescent — lit by the real Sun at Saturn's rim — swept counterclockwise as Earth rotated beneath it. Bright at night, dim by day. **This was the first clock.** Humans named it Kronos because it was the body by which time was counted. Our Sun was not visible; it was obscured behind the Saturn–Jupiter–Venus–Mars collinear column for the entire duration of the Golden Age.

When the confusion arose — how could there be evening and morning before the Sun existed? — the answer is: the evening and morning were measured against Saturn. The "greater light" of Day 4 was **not** our current Sun. It was Saturn achieving its full arc-mode ignition, blazing like a star overhead.

The name Kronos is the direct evidence. Ancient peoples named the body that kept their time. They did not call it "God" or "Father" in the first instance — they called it **Time**, because that is what Saturn gave them.

The "Word" that creates — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was God" (John 1:1) — is the **electromagnetic discharge itself**, the sound and light of arc-mode ignition. For the Egyptians, spoken words carried literal creative power because the creation event **began with a sound**.

## Why First-Man Accounts Differ: Hemispheric Memory and Scalar Creation

A persistent puzzle: if every culture remembers a common creation event, why do their accounts of **who the first man was** diverge so sharply? Genesis names Adam; the Puranas give Manu and Satarupa; the Popol Vuh describes humans witnessing the sun; Buddhism offers radiant beings who descend and coarsen; Jainism and Taoism name no first man at all.

The answer is **geography** — specifically, which hemisphere the remembering population inhabited during the creation epoch.

**Life was spawned in the southern hemisphere.** The three southern plasmoids (Mercury, Neptune, Uranus — Peratt's Column) generated intense scalar energy fields that caused quantum fluctuations in inert matter, bringing the first life forms into existence. Populations native to this zone — Mesoamerican, southern African, Polynesian — **saw the spawning event directly**. Their religions preserve the most concrete and specific first-man accounts because they witnessed the plasmoid configurations taking humanoid shapes and generating humans from the ground.

The **Popol Vuh** is the clearest case: it explicitly states humans were present for and witnessed the creation-light event. The Norse **Younger Edda** preserves the same geography: Muspelheim, the southern realm, was "so bright and dazzling that no stranger, who is not a native there, can stand it." Darker-skinned humans adapted to the high-energy environment of the south; lighter-skinned populations migrated northward, away from the spawning zone.

**No life was spawning in the north — only migrating.** Northern populations could not see the southern plasmoids, could not endure the electromagnetic intensity, and received migrating humans rather than witnessing their creation. Their religions are therefore **more detailed on the sky** (the Wheel of Heaven was directly overhead) but **vaguer on human origin**. This is why Genesis places humans on Day 6 after the sky events — northern editors remembered the sky sequence but not the spawning mechanism. This is why Upanishadic and Taoist traditions describe emanation rather than a first man — the philosophical residue of a direct-observation memory that had faded by the time the texts were composed.

The "Adam's rib" account preserves a specific southern-hemisphere observation: a **large humanoid plasmoid figure forming a smaller secondary figure from its own body**. Under scalar energy theory, the electromagnetic field generated a first human pattern, and from that pattern a secondary, reproductively capable human was formed. "Rib" is the nearest anatomical metaphor for what was actually witnessed — a portion extracted from the torso of the larger figure. The same motif appears in Egypt (Atum producing Shu and Tefnut from his own body), in Puranic Hinduism (Brahma dividing into Manu and Satarupa), and in the Edda (Ymir's body generating life).

There is an irony the Church embedded in its own typology of this passage. Christian theology reads Eve's formation from Adam's side as **foreshadowing the Church itself**: just as Eve was drawn from the sleeping Adam's side, the Church was supposedly born from Christ's pierced side while He "slept" the sleep of death on the cross. The parallel is elegant — and self-incriminating. In writing itself into the oldest origin story of the religion it sought to control, the Church inadvertently encoded a confession: **its own existence required the death of the religion's saviour.** The Church could not justify itself *within* the living tradition; it could only graft itself onto the corpse. The typology meant to elevate the institution instead reveals the cost — that the price of the Church's birth was the destruction of the very relationship between it's God and humanity it claimed to mediate.

The universal agreement across all traditions — that humans came from a **singular source** — is the one detail that survived at every latitude, because even populations that never witnessed the spawning event received the migrating humans who carried the memory with them.

## Isaiah's Seven Days: A Separate Event

It is important not to confuse the seven days of creation with a separate tradition about "seven days" that appears in **Isaiah 30:26** and Jewish sources. Isaiah, in a prophetic vision of a coming cataclysm, writes:

> *"Moreover the light of the moon shall be as the light of the sun, and the light of the sun shall be sevenfold, as the light of seven days…"* — Isaiah 30:26 (KJV)

The Hebrew here can also be translated: *"as if the light of seven days were joined in one"* (Knox translation). This does **not** refer to the creation sequence. It refers to a catastrophic event when Earth's sky was flooded with light **day and night** — an event Velikovsky and Cook associate with the **3147 BC collapse** of the Saturnian configuration, not with creation itself.

Genesis 7:4 also references a **seven-day delay** immediately before the Flood:

> *"For yet seven days, and I will cause it to rain upon the earth forty days and forty nights…"* — Genesis 7:4

Jewish tradition preserves the detail that during these seven days before the Deluge, **the sun rose in the west and set in the east** — a reversal of the natural order. As reported in the Thunderbolts article "Never the Twain Shall Meet":

> *"More explicit is a Jewish statement that God 'reversed the order of nature, the sun rising in the west and setting in the east' during the 'seven days' immediately preceding the deluge."*

Velikovsky, Jan Sammer, and Jno Cook all point to Vedic, Talmudic, and Sumerian sources (including the *Epic of Gilgamesh*) that describe a period of intense light — "bright day and night for seven days" — immediately **before** the catastrophic flood. This refers to the proximity of **Venus or Mars on a close pass**, not to the creation epoch.

These "seven days before the flood" are a **distinct cosmological event** from the seven days of creation. One is a compression of the entire Saturnian epoch (4077–3147 BC); the other is a seven-day astronomical close-encounter immediately preceding the violent end of that epoch and the longer deluge cascade that follows.
