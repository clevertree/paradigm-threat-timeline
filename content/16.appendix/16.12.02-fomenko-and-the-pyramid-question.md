# Fomenko and the Pyramid Question

Fomenko's work on the pyramids is a case study in what happens when a brilliant analytical framework collides with an incomplete cosmological model. His contribution to the pyramid question — the geopolymer evidence, the debunking of Scaligerian dating, the identification of concrete construction — is invaluable. His answer to *why* pyramids exist is not.

## What Fomenko Got Right

Fomenko's core contributions to the pyramid question are difficult to dispute:

1. **Geopolymer concrete is real.** Davidovich's chemical proof, independently verified, demonstrates that pyramid blocks are cast artificial stone. This eliminates the absurd "millions of slaves dragging blocks" narrative.
2. **Iron and steel were used.** Steel tools were found in the masonry. Herodotus records iron tools. This is incompatible with a "Bronze Age" dating.
3. **Pyramids are not tombs.** No body was found in the Great Pyramid. The unmarked sarcophagus resembles a treasure chest. Gold coins were reportedly found inside.
4. **Herodotus describes moulds.** Read without Egyptological bias, his text describes wooden casings for casting — not ramps for dragging.
5. **The technology was lost.** Geopolymer concrete, like Damascus steel, was a classified technique destroyed when the Empire fragmented.

## Where Fomenko Falls Short

### The Dating Problem

Fomenko places pyramid construction in the XIV–XVI centuries CE — part of the Horde's monumental building programme alongside Hagia Sophia, the Great Wall, and Gothic cathedrals. This is a reasonable inference *within his framework*, which compresses all "ancient" history into the medieval period. But Fomenko cannot prove the pyramids aren't older than his framework allows. His statistical methods detect chronological shifts in *textual records* — they cannot directly date stone structures. The pyramids could have been built millennia earlier using techniques Fomenko correctly identified but incorrectly dated.

### The Purpose Problem

Fomenko offers multiple speculative explanations for why the pyramids were built, none of them satisfying:

- **Christian Easter cakes:** The three pyramids represent the Holy Trinity; the word "pyramid" derives from Greek *pyramis* (honey-wheat cake shaped like a cone). Pyramidions were painted white and housed the "Sun God" — i.e., Christ.
- **Horde treasury vaults:** The Great Pyramid stored gold reserves as an emergency reserve stock for the Empire.
- **Scythian burial mounds:** Pyramids are Scythian *kurgans* scaled up — the "pinnacle of mound architecture." Some mounds served as churches (e.g., Tsarsky Kurgan in Crimea).

Each of these explanations addresses only the **three Giza pyramids**. None explains why pyramids are found across Mesoamerica, China, Sudan, Indonesia, Bosnia, and the Pacific. None addresses the precise equatorial/ley-line placement. A Christian-Horde monument programme does not produce identical structures among cultures with no contact with the Rus.

### The Cosmological Blind Spot

Fomenko does not engage with Saturnian Cosmology, plasma physics, or the electromagnetic properties of pyramid geometry. He is a topologist and statistician — his tools are textual analysis, astronomical dating, and pattern recognition in chronicles. He does not ask what the pyramids *do*, only who built them and when. This leaves the most important question — their function — unanswered within his framework.

The Saturnian model provides what Fomenko's model lacks: a reason for pyramids to exist in the first place. During the Dark Ages, catastrophic electromagnetic discharges between closely-orbiting planets required structures capable of channelling and grounding enormous currents. Pyramid geometry — oriented to cardinal directions, placed on electromagnetic nodes, constructed with piezoelectric materials — is an engineering response to a cosmic electrical environment that no longer exists.

## The Soviet Constraint

Fomenko published his first chronological work in 1973, under the Soviet system, and continued through the collapse of the USSR and into post-Soviet Russia. His framework is rigorously materialist — history is texts, dates, and statistical patterns. There is no room for lost civilisations, planetary cataclysms, or pre-diluvian technology. This is not necessarily a personal failing; it may reflect the survival instinct of an academic who wanted his books to outlast the political fires. Soviet and post-Soviet academia did not reward speculative cosmology. Fomenko stayed within bounds that would let his work be published, peer-reviewed, and preserved.

The result is a framework that can identify *what was lied about* but not *what was lost*. Fomenko can demonstrate that the conventional chronology is fabricated, but cannot reconstruct the deeper past that the fabrication was designed to conceal — because his tools only reach as far back as the written record, and the written record itself is the product of the cover-up.

## This Timeline's Synthesis

This timeline uses Fomenko's evidence while rejecting his dating and purpose claims for the pyramids:

- **Construction method:** Geopolymer concrete — accepted (Davidovich evidence is compelling)
- **Dating:** Dark Ages, not XIV–XVI century CE — the knowledge is Golden Age inheritance, not medieval invention
- **Purpose:** Electromagnetic cathodic protection during planetary cataclysm — not Christian symbolism or imperial treasuries
- **Global distribution:** Explained by a global civilisation responding to a global electromagnetic crisis, not by a single medieval empire's building programme

Fomenko cracked the code on *how*. The *why* and *when* require a cosmological framework he was never in a position to adopt.
