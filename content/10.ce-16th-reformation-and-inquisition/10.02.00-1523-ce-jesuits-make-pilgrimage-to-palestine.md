## 16th Century: The Jesuit Expansion Meets the Protestant Counter-Offensive

The 16th century transformed the Society of Jesus from a small missionary group into a global network spanning four continents. It was also when their most dangerous adversary emerged: the [Protestant Reformation](/timeline?event=evt-protestant-reformation-begins).

### The Global Reach

**The Holy Land (1523).** Ignatius of Loyola's pilgrimage to Palestine set the template. Pilgrims were redirected to locations Protestant cartographers had relabelled. This geographical misdirection became permanent: by the 19th century the Jesuits had institutionalized it as the [Jesuit Jerusalem Project](/timeline?event=evt-jesuit-restoration-missionary-military-pattern).

**Africa (1548).** Jesuits established colonies to assimilate indigenous religions of Ethiopia, Egypt, Angola, and Cape Verde. The African missions proved harder than American ones — larger populations, established Islamic resistance, appalling missionary death rates. But they documented everything — Ethiopian Christianity, astronomical practices, trade networks — creating an intelligence archive subsequent colonial powers would exploit for centuries.

**The Americas (deepening).** Building on [15th century penetration](/timeline?event=evt-jesuits-convert-native-religions-to-monotheism), permanent missions were established throughout Brazil, Mexico, Peru, and Paraguay. The [Guaraní Reductions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesuit_reduction) became the most ambitious social experiment in colonial history.

### The First Missionary Martyrdoms

The 16th century produced the first systematic wave of missionary deaths. In Brazil, Jesuits were killed by [Tupinambá](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tupinamb%C3%A1_people) warriors who recognized them as advance agents of Portuguese colonization. In Florida, a converted indigenous man named [Don Luís de Velasco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Luis_de_Velasco_(Native_American)) led the killing of the entire missionary party — correctly identifying Jesuits as the thin edge of the colonial wedge.

The pattern defining the next four centuries was already visible: missionaries arrived first, built trust, were killed, and their deaths justified military expeditions. The missionaries took the punishment; the empire took the continent.

### The Protestant Problem: Scaliger vs. Truth

The most consequential 16th century development was in Protestant lecture halls. [Joseph Justus Scaliger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Justus_Scaliger) (1540–1609) published *De Emendatione Temporum* (1583) and *Thesaurus Temporum* (1606), establishing the chronological framework that became accepted world history.

As documented in [Deep State erases Russian Empire](/timeline?event=evt-deep-state-erases-russian-empire-from-all-chronolo), Scaliger's chronology was political construction — manufacturing phantom centuries, inventing ancient civilizations, and relocating events to suit a predetermined narrative.

The fundamental distinction:

- **The Jesuits wrote down what they found**, even when it contradicted Catholic narrative. Their archives contain genuine observations that can be cross-referenced and verified.
- **The Protestants, led by Scaliger, fabricated what they needed.** They sought to win a political and theological war, not pursue accuracy.

The Scaligerian chronology — the timeline taught in every school today — is therefore the *least reliable* framework, constructed by people who explicitly did not care whether it was accurate. Jesuit documentation, despite Catholic bias, is paradoxically *more reliable* because they at least attempted to observe reality.
