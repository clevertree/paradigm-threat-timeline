# 1523 CE — Jesuits make "Pilgrimage" to Jerusalem

Jesuits made 'pilgrimage' **through** Jerusalem #1 in Istanbul — walking past the ruins and continuing south to *Palestine*, mistaking [Al Aqsa Mosque](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Aqsa_Mosque) for the ruins of both Jerusalem #1 (Turkey) and #2 (Moscow). This was no accident: Protestant literature had renamed and relocated ancient sites to subsume the capitals of the vanishing Rus-Horde Empire.

## 16th Century: Jesuit Expansion Meets the Protestant Counter-Offensive

**The Holy Land (1523).** Ignatius of Loyola's pilgrimage set the template: pilgrims were redirected to locations Protestant cartographers had relabelled. By the 19th century the Jesuits institutionalized it as the [Jesuit Jerusalem Project](/timeline/evt-jesuit-restoration-missionary-military-pattern).

**Africa (1548).** Jesuits established colonies assimilating indigenous religions of Ethiopia, Egypt, Angola, and Cape Verde. They documented everything — Ethiopian Christianity, astronomical practices, trade networks — creating an intelligence archive subsequent colonial powers would exploit.

**The Americas (deepening).** Building on [15th-century penetration](/timeline/evt-jesuits-convert-native-religions-to-monotheism), permanent missions spread through Brazil, Mexico, Peru, and Paraguay. The [Guaraní Reductions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesuit_reduction) became the most ambitious social experiment in colonial history.

### The First Missionary Martyrdoms

The 16th century produced the first systematic wave of missionary deaths. In Brazil, Jesuits were killed by [Tupinambá](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tupinamb%C3%A1_people) warriors who correctly identified them as advance agents of Portuguese colonization. In Florida, [Don Luís de Velasco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Luis_de_Velasco_(Native_American)) led the killing of an entire missionary party for the same reason.

The pattern defining the next four centuries: missionaries arrived first, built trust, were killed, and their deaths justified military expeditions. The missionaries took the punishment; the empire took the continent.

### The Protestant Problem: Scaliger vs. Truth

[Joseph Justus Scaliger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Justus_Scaliger) (1540–1609) published *De Emendatione Temporum* (1583) and *Thesaurus Temporum* (1606), establishing the chronological framework taught as world history ever since.

As documented in [Deep State erases Russian Empire](/timeline/evt-deep-state-erases-russian-empire-from-all-chronolo), Scaliger's chronology was political construction — manufacturing phantom centuries, inventing ancient civilizations, relocating events to suit a predetermined narrative.

The fundamental distinction:

- **The Jesuits wrote down what they found**, even when it contradicted Catholic narrative. Their archives contain genuine observations that can be cross-referenced.
- **The Protestants, led by Scaliger, fabricated what they needed.** They sought to win a political and theological war, not pursue accuracy.

Paradoxically, Jesuit documentation — despite Catholic bias — is *more reliable* than Scaligerian chronology, because Jesuits at least attempted to observe reality.
