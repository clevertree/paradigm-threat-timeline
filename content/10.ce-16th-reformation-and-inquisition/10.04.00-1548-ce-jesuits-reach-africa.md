# 1548 CE — Jesuits reach Africa

See [Jesuit Operations (15th c.)](/timeline/evt-jesuits-convert-native-religions-to-monotheism) and [The Jesuit Arc](/timeline/evt-jesuits-make-pilgrimage-to-palestine) for the full timeline. The Jesuit expansion slowly continued south to [Africa](https://www.creighton.edu/sites/default/files/2022-01/35-Africa.pdf). Missionary stations preceded colonial administrators and the [transatlantic slave trade](/timeline/evt-slave-trades-horde-fracture) infrastructure. Jesuits established colonies in an attempt to assimilate indigenous religions of Ethiopia, Egypt, the area of the Zambesi River, Angola and Cape Verde.
