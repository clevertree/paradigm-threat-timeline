# The Oprichnina

The [Oprichnina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprichnina) (1564–1572) is conventionally described as Ivan the Terrible's reign of terror against the boyar nobility. The New Chronology [inverts this](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/06_07.html): the Oprichnina was a *dynastic coup* staged by the Zakharyins-Romanovs — relatives of Ivan's first wife Anastasia — who seized government while the legitimate czar-khan was incapacitated, then used state apparatus to annihilate their Hordian rivals.

*The actual authors of the Oprichnina were the Zakharyins-Romanovs, who ruled on behalf of an infant czar and used the apparatus to exterminate the Hordian military elite.*

## The Four Ivans

**"Ivan the Terrible" is not one man** but a composite of four czar-khans:

| Czar | Reign (NC) | Key Fact |
|---|---|---|
| **Ivan IV Vasilyevich** | 1547–1553 | Conquered Kazan 1552; fell gravely ill 1553, became Vasiliy the Blessed |
| **Dmitry Ivanovich** | 1553–1563 | Infant son; ruled by council; began Livonian War |
| **Ivan V Ivanovich** | 1563–1572 | Young son; Zakharyins-Romanovs hold real power — Oprichnina unleashed |
| **Simeon-Ivan Beckbulatovitch** | 1572–1584 | Hordian Khan; restored after Horde defeated Zakharyin faction |

## The Coup

In 1553 Ivan IV was struck by severe illness (he is *Vasiliy the Blessed*). His infant son Dmitry acceded under a governing council. The Livonian War launched against the Western Reformation. In 1563 Dmitry died; younger brother **infant Ivan V** became czar. The Zakharyins-Romanovs seized the court and sabotaged the Livonian War.

## The Terror: 1564–1572

The Oprichnina established a parallel state. By 1568 mass executions began and chronicles ceased — chroniclers were killed. In **1569–1570**, the old Hordian capital **Veliky Novgorod = Yaroslavl** was destroyed, its population banished. The most consequential act: deliberate erasure of the Horde dynasty's power base and records. The name "Veliky Novgorod" was stripped from Yaroslavl and transferred to a small Pskov settlement.

> *"The new people came to power — the Zakharyins (the Skharievtsy), the future Romanovs. They wished to distort the Russian history so that the origins of the old Horde dynasty from Yaroslavl were concealed."*

## The Esther-Judith Parallel

The NC identifies the Oprichnina as the historical original of **Esther and Judith** — both describe coups via a woman who infiltrates the court and engineers massacre of the ruling class. In XVI century, Esther's role is played by **Marfa Sobakina**, Ivan's third wife. Purim commemorates the massacre of the Hordian boyar class.

## The Horde Counterattack

The Hordian army — recorded as the "Crimean Khan's invasion" — struck back. In 1571 the Horde burned Moscow. The Horde prevailed: Zakharyin oprichnina leaders were executed. **Malyuta Skuratov-Belsky**, conventionally Ivan's enforcer, was actually a *Hordian* officer hunting down Zakharyin leadership. In 1572 Ivan V abdicated and Hordian khan **Simeon Beckbulatovitch** was crowned.

## Romanov Falsification

Once the Romanovs seized the throne in XVII century, they: merged four czars into one to make the coup invisible; erased their role in the terror; stripped "Veliky Novgorod" from Yaroslavl to their northwest homeland; suppressed the Esther/Judith narrative implicating Anastasia Romanova's family; and declared the Horde dynasty illegitimate — rebranding three centuries of Russian-Hordian rule as a "foreign Tatar yoke."
