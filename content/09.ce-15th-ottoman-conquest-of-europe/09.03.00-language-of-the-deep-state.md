## New Chronology: Latin Was Invented by Imperial Reformists

**Latin was created in the 15th–17th centuries, not antiquity.** The "ancient" Latin and Greek were *"created later, in the epoch of the dissolution of the Empire, based mainly on Church Slavonic"* ([ch.4:4](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/04_4.html)). Reformists invented languages to *"build barriers between populations of newly-formed states"* ([ch.7:8](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/07_08.html)). In XIX–XX centuries scholars mistakenly dated Latin to deep antiquity ([ch.8:21](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/08_21.html)).

**Latin appeared on Rus-Horde artefacts** — including a 1618 sabre by Russian craftsman Ilya Prosvit ([ch.7:8](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/07_08.html)). This marks it as a *liturgical/administrative language*, not the mother tongue.

**Rus-Horde was called "Latinia"** by some sources, possibly from Russian *LYUDNAYA* ("populous") ([ch.3:7](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/03_7.html)). The "Latini" were a **political faction** linked to heresy and Western power — a deep-state formation tied to the Empire's fragmentation.

**Petrarch used Latin as a redaction tool** — deliberately *"obscuring the Middle Ages by substituting it with 'classical antiquity'"* ([ch.5:30](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/05_30.html)). Latin functioned as a redaction instrument before Scaliger formalised the chronology.

**Latin derives from Church Slavonic.** Fomenko traced over 15,800 "foreign" words to ~3,570 Slavonic root groups ([ch.8:21](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/08_21.html)). The "dead language" preserved across institutions is the **living signature of imperial administration**.

## The Martian Origin Hypothesis

If Latin originated on Mars and was transmitted by a breakaway civilisation, we would expect gradual appearance during imperial expansion, tied to technological uplift, imperfectly adopted — precisely Fomenko's fingerprint. **Vulgar Latin** — dozens of corrupted variants — is structural evidence of *failed transmission*: each Romance language shows where an Earth population tried to absorb a language that didn't fit their syntax.

Wherever Latin appears in Fomenko's reconstruction, it arrives alongside military conquest, institutional construction, and systematic naming of every science — as if a **knowledge transfer** was underway with Latin as the indexing language. An Earthly origin cannot explain why a dead city-state's language indexes all human science. A Martian-origin model explains it directly.

The [British Empire](/timeline/evt-british-empire-as-deep-state-instrument) inherited this template: the British never spoke Latin, yet their framework — *habeas corpus*, *Cestui Que Vie*, *Rex* — operates in Latin, with the London–D.C.–Vatican triangle using it as shared ceremonial tongue.

The standard narrative asks us to believe a language spoken by a small Italian population died in the 5th century and spontaneously became every institution's administrative language out of scholastic nostalgia. The Martian-origin model asks only that the civilisation transmitting advanced knowledge also transmitted the indexing language. **Latin is not dead. It is the operational language of a non-Earthly administration — still in use.**
