## New Chronology: Latin Was Invented by Imperial Reformists

**Latin was created in the 15th–17th centuries, not antiquity.** "Ancient" Latin and Greek were *"created later, in the epoch of the dissolution of the Empire, based mainly on Church Slavonic"* ([ch.4:4](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/04_4.html)). Reformists invented languages to *"build barriers between populations of newly-formed states"* ([ch.7:8](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/07_08.html)).

**Latin on Rus-Horde artefacts** — including a 1618 sabre by craftsman Ilya Prosvit ([ch.7:8](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/07_08.html)) — marks it as *liturgical/administrative*, not a mother tongue.

**Rus-Horde was called "Latinia"** by some sources, possibly from Russian *LYUDNAYA* ("populous") ([ch.3:7](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/03_7.html)). The "Latini" were a **political faction** tied to the Empire's fragmentation.

**Petrarch used Latin as a redaction tool** — *"obscuring the Middle Ages by substituting it with 'classical antiquity'"* ([ch.5:30](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/05_30.html)).

**Latin derives from Church Slavonic.** Fomenko traced over 15,800 "foreign" words to ~3,570 Slavonic root groups ([ch.8:21](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/08_21.html)). The "dead language" preserved across institutions is the **living signature of imperial administration**.

## Fiction That Remembers the Martian-Latin Connection

Science fiction encodes Latin as the language of a breakaway civilisation: **_A Trip to Mars_** (Rossi, 1920) — Martians speak Latin; **_War of the Worlds_** (Wells, 1898) — Martians mirror Latin-speaking authority; **_Pilgrims_** (Leonard, 2024) — aliens arrive speaking Latin via the Vatican. The pattern: Latin = the language of those who arrive from above.

## The Martian Origin Hypothesis

If Latin originated on Mars, we would expect gradual appearance during imperial expansion, tied to technological uplift, imperfectly adopted — precisely Fomenko's fingerprint. **Vulgar Latin** — dozens of corrupted variants — is structural evidence of *failed transmission*: each Romance language shows where an Earth population tried to absorb a language that didn't fit their syntax.

Wherever Latin appears in Fomenko's reconstruction, it arrives alongside military conquest, institutional construction, and systematic naming of every science — as if a **knowledge transfer** was underway with Latin as the indexing language. The [British Empire](/timeline/evt-british-empire-as-deep-state-instrument) inherited this template: Britain never spoke Latin, yet its framework — *habeas corpus*, *Cestui Que Vie*, *Rex* — operates in it, with the London–D.C.–Vatican triangle using Latin as shared ceremonial tongue.

## Conclusion

The standard narrative asks us to believe a small Italian population's language died in the 5th century and spontaneously became every institution's administrative language out of scholastic nostalgia. The Martian-origin model asks only that the civilisation transmitting advanced knowledge also transmitted the indexing language. **Latin is not dead. It is the operational language of a non-Earthly administration — still in use.**
