## Disease as Weapon of Authority

The Apocalypse Crusade's stated justification was eradicating **venereal plagues** spread by the [bacchanalian cult](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/05_08.html#the9) Fomenko identifies as Western Christianity's dominant form in XIII–XV centuries. Ceremonial prostitution was part of Western Christian liturgy; brothels operated openly, immortalized in [Pompeii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pompeii)'s sexual frescoes — which Fomenko dates to XV–XVI century, not "79 AD."

Diseases — syphilis chief among them — traveled back to Rus-Horde Empire along its own trade routes. The Khans made a drastic decision:

> *The Khans of the Empire found themselves confronting a serious state problem… the khans made the only, as they thought, correct decision — to wipe out the diseased population using military force. To burn down the houses of the sick.*
> — Fomenko & Nosovskiy, [*How It Was In Reality*, Ch. 5 §38](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/05_38.html)

The **pattern** matters more than specifics: disease spreads, fear is magnified, panic produces sharp reordering of authority benefiting those who spread the fear. This template repeats: 1665 Great Plague consolidated Restoration authority; 1918 Spanish Flu enabled post-war global governance; COVID-19 produced unprecedented centralization. Emergency powers invoked, populations sorted into "clean" and "unclean," authority consolidates upward.

## End of Free Gnostic Christianity

Before the Crusade, dominant Christianity in many regions was **free and gnostic** — personal revelation, no priestly intermediary. The Crusade ended this. Christianity was remade to **require priests and doctors for salvation**: if sick, only Church's healers could save you; if spiritually "unclean," only priests could absolve. Salvation became **institutional product**.
