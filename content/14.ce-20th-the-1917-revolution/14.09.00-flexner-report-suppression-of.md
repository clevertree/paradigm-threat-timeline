## The Flexner Report (1910)

Abraham Flexner, a schoolteacher with no medical degree, was commissioned by the **Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching** to evaluate all 155 medical schools in the United States and Canada. His report, published in 1910, recommended that medical education be standardized around laboratory science, pharmaceutical intervention, and surgery. Schools that did not meet his criteria — including all **13 homeopathic medical schools**, most eclectic schools, and the majority of Black medical colleges — were defunded and closed within two decades.

The Flexner Report is not contested. It is taught in medical history courses as a progressive reform. What is less commonly acknowledged is **who funded the reform and what it excluded:**

- **John D. Rockefeller** and the General Education Board provided the bulk of implementation funding. Rockefeller had recently acquired major pharmaceutical interests.
- **Andrew Carnegie** funded Flexner's study through the Carnegie Foundation.
- Schools that taught electrotherapy, vitalism, homeopathy, or any model of "energy in the body" were systematically defunded. Not disproven — **defunded**.

The survivors became the modern medical school system: drug-and-surgery-based, pharmaceutical-industry-aligned, and structurally hostile to any paradigm that treats the body as an electrical system.
