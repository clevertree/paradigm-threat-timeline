# 1900 CE — 20th Century: The Reverse Crusades Continue

The early 20th century brought the fulfilment of Marx's prophecy — or rather, the execution of the script. In 1917, the Bolshevik Revolution swept away the Romanov dynasty and, with it, the last institutional connections to the old Rus-Horde. The royal family was allegedly executed in Yekaterinburg in 1918, though no definitive proof of their deaths has ever been produced. What is certain is that an entire civilisation was incinerated: its churches, its documents, its communal land systems, its aristocratic memory-keepers, and its independent scholars.

## The Reverse Crusade Framework

The 20th century's two "World Wars" are Reverse Crusades — pan-European coalitions marching into Russia, repeating the pattern established in 1812 ([Reverse Crusade I](/timeline/evt-napoleon-invades-russia)):

| Designation | Conventional Name | Date | Coalition → Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reverse Crusade I** | Napoleonic Wars | 1812 | All of Europe → Russia |
| **Reverse Crusade II** | "World War I" | 1914 | Central Powers + Ottoman Empire destabilize Russia; revolution follows |
| **Reverse Crusade III** | "World War II" | 1941 | Nazi Germany + Axis → Soviet Union (Operation Barbarossa) |
| **Reverse Crusade IV** | NATO expansion / Ukraine proxy war | 2014– | Western financial bloc → Russia (ongoing) |

The numbering "World War I / II" is itself an obfuscation — it implies each war was a separate, unprecedented event when in reality they are episodes of the same centuries-long campaign to destroy the Horde's successors. See [Aftermath of 1812](/timeline/evt-aftermath-of-1812-russian-pursuit-to-paris) for the origin of this framework.

**Reverse Crusade II** (1914–1918) weaponized Russia's internal weakness. The war's Western Front is presented as the main event, but the Eastern Front achieved the strategic objective: total collapse of the Russian state, enabling the Bolshevik coup. Germany transported Lenin to Russia in a sealed train. Britain and France — nominally Russia's *allies* — did nothing to prevent the revolution and swiftly recognized the new regime that destroyed everything the alliance supposedly protected.

**Reverse Crusade III** (1941–1945) was the most direct repetition of 1812: Hitler assembled a pan-European army (German, Italian, Romanian, Hungarian, Finnish, Croatian, Slovak, Spanish volunteers) and marched it into Russia. Like Napoleon, Hitler chose Russia over England — see [The Napoleon–Britain Paradox](/timeline/evt-napoleonic-wars). Like Napoleon, his army was destroyed by the Russian interior. Like 1812, the Russian army pursued the invaders all the way back to their capital.

This chapter examines 1917 not as proletarian triumph but as the **final act of a centuries-long campaign** to destroy the remnants of the world-empire. The Romanovs began the demolition in 1613; 19th-century ideologues provided the blueprint; the Bolsheviks finished the job. The revolution systematically destroyed the Orthodox Church (manuscripts, genealogies), aristocratic families, village communes, document archives, and the independent intellectual class — not collateral damage but the objective. Events covered: February/October 1917, the Romanov fate, Kinkel's flight, intellectual emigration, and Soviet historiography as the final layer of falsification.
