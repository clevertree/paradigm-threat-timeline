# Nuclear Energy and the "Bomb": The Most Expensive Way to Boil Water

## The Official Story

In 1938, Otto Hahn and Fritz Strassmann [discovered nuclear fission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_nuclear_fission) — the splitting of a uranium atom into lighter elements, releasing enormous energy. By 1942, the [Manhattan Project](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manhattan_Project) had marshalled 130,000 workers and $2 billion (≈$28 billion today) to weaponize this discovery. On 16 July 1945, the Trinity test detonated the first nuclear device in the New Mexico desert. Three weeks later, "Little Boy" and "Fat Man" were dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, killing an estimated 100,000–200,000 people and ending World War II. The world entered the Atomic Age. The Cold War arms race followed, with the United States and Soviet Union each stockpiling tens of thousands of warheads capable of ending civilization — or so the story goes.

What is fabricated is the **story built on top of these facts** — a story in which nuclear material is uniquely dangerous, nuclear bombs are civilization-ending weapons, and nuclear "waste" is an unsolvable existential crisis.

## What Nuclear Energy Actually Is

Nuclear physicist **Galen Windsor** (1926–2008) worked at Hanford under GE from the late 1940s, helped design fuel-handling systems for multiple reactor facilities, and held a nuclear fuel handler license. In lectures he gave across the United States during the 1980s and 1990s — [still available on video](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=galen+windsor+nuclear+scare+scam) — Windsor demonstrated what nuclear energy actually is at the operational level:

- **Reactors are steam plants.** The nuclear component heats water. The water makes steam. The steam turns turbines. "The most expensive and effective way to boil water," as Windsor put it.
- **He handled enriched fuel with bare hands** throughout his career at Hanford. "When we were first working with nuclear material — uranium, plutonium — we held it in our hands. If some of it got hot, we'd throw it in the water."
- **He swam in the spent-fuel cooling pool** — the very water the fuel rods were submerged in. He later drank from it daily. He suffered no ill effects.
- **In his public lectures, he ate radioactive material on camera** — licking it off his fingers in front of audiences, explaining he had done so for years.
- **He was present at Hanford during the startup era** and stated that Tom Hall, the Hanford manager, personally knew Enrico Fermi and was present when the B Reactor went critical in 1944.

Windsor was not a crank. He held verified credentials, worked at real facilities, and his claims about Hanford operations are corroborated by public records. The [Harold McCluskey incident](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harold_McCluskey) (1976) — in which a Hanford worker survived an americium exposure 500 times the occupational limit, lived eleven more years, and died of heart disease with **no cancer found at autopsy** — independently confirms Windsor's central claim: enriched nuclear material is far less dangerous than the public has been told.

## "Toxic Waste" vs. Depleted Uranium: The Critical Distinction

The nuclear narrative deliberately conflates two completely different substances:

### Spent Nuclear Fuel ("Toxic Waste")

What the industry calls "nuclear waste" is **spent fuel** — enriched uranium that has been through a reactor cycle. It remains highly valuable. Windsor stated each ton was worth approximately $10 million in recoverable isotopes. Multiple countries — France, the UK, Russia, Japan, India — **reprocess** spent fuel, extracting usable material and recycling it. The United States built a reprocessing facility — the [GE Morris Operation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morris_Operation) in Grundy County, Illinois — but never operated it. Ford halted reprocessing in 1976; Carter banned it in 1977. The Barnwell facility in South Carolina, completed the same year, was never permitted to open.

The result: 772 tons of spent fuel sit in a pool at Morris, Illinois. Tens of thousands more tons sit in pools and dry casks at reactor sites across the country. This material is not "waste" — it is **inventory** that the government has forbidden the industry from using. Windsor's claim that the nuclear waste crisis is manufactured becomes considerably harder to dismiss when the government's own actions created it.

### Depleted Uranium (DU)

[Depleted uranium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Depleted_uranium) is the **opposite** of spent fuel. It is what remains after the useful isotopes (U-235) have been stripped away through enrichment. DU is 99.8% U-238 — a dense, heavy metal that is 40% less radioactive than natural uranium but remains **chemically toxic** as a heavy metal.

DU is genuinely harmful:

- **Immunotoxic, teratogenic, neurotoxic**, and potentially carcinogenic according to peer-reviewed research
- **315–350 tons** were used in munitions during the 1991 Gulf War alone
- **Fallujah, Iraq** recorded the "highest rate of genetic damage in any population ever studied" after exposure to DU munitions
- Gulf War veterans exposed to DU showed **14 times** the chromosome abnormalities of unexposed veterans
- DU burns on impact, creating **fine aerosol particles** that can be inhaled — this is the genuinely dangerous exposure vector

The nuclear narrative reverses reality: it tells the public that **enriched material** (spent fuel) is lethally dangerous while saying almost nothing about **depleted uranium** — the material that actually causes documented harm when weaponized. Windsor ate enriched material on camera and lived to old age. Populations exposed to DU munitions show catastrophic health effects. The labels are inverted.

## Hiroshima and Nagasaki: What Actually Happened

The official narrative claims two single bombs — one uranium, one plutonium — each produced yields of 15–21 kilotons, vaporizing tens of thousands of people instantly and producing radiation effects lasting generations.

The evidence tells a different story:

1. **The damage pattern matches firebombing, not a single-point detonation.** Tokyo was firebombed flat in March 1945 with conventional incendiaries. The destruction at Hiroshima and Nagasaki is visually comparable. Multiple claims in test footage analysis show **two superimposed smoke plumes** made to look like one — a compositing technique used elsewhere in alleged nuclear test footage.

2. **Vegetation returned within weeks.** Flowers and plants began growing in Hiroshima within a month of the bombing. If nuclear radiation persisted at the levels claimed — contamination lasting "hundreds or thousands of years" — this would be impossible. Hiroshima is a bustling city today. People live, work, and grow food there.

3. **No credible evidence of "vaporized" humans.** The famous "shadow" photographs — imprints supposedly left by people who were instantaneously vaporized — are presented as proof of unprecedented destructive power. The physical mechanism claimed (a human body converted entirely to plasma by a single flash, leaving a photographic negative on stone) has never been demonstrated or replicated. These shadows are consistent with **incendiary flash effects** — phosphorus-based compounds produce intense white light that bleaches or scorches surfaces differentially around obstacles.

4. **The yield is consistent with a small DU device enhanced with incendiary material.** A small-yield depleted uranium device combined with phosphorus-based incendiary powder would explain: the visible flash, the blast damage consistent with conventional bombing, the rapid return of vegetation, and the lack of persistent radiation. The 15–21 kiloton figure is a claim, not a measured fact — it was calculated after the event by the same military apparatus that conducted the bombing.

5. **Nuclear test footage does not survive scrutiny.** Analysis of declassified test footage reveals: cameras surviving at distances where buildings are destroyed, blast waves arriving from behind the camera, identical trees appearing in footage attributed to different tests, and scale inconsistencies suggesting model work. As one analyst observed: "Back then they thought King Kong looked real in the movie. Now we can see it's clearly fake." The earliest test footage used **models and stacked dynamite**; later tests used large conventional explosive charges that produce the characteristic mushroom cloud shape without any nuclear component.
