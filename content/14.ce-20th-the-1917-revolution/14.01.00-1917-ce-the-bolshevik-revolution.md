## October 1917: The Bolsheviks Execute the Plan

The October Revolution brought the Bolsheviks to power under Lenin. What followed was systematic application of the Marxist demolition script:

**Phase 1: Destroy the Church.** Thousands of churches demolished, repurposed, or sealed. Manuscripts burned, icons melted for gold, monks and priests shot or sent to camps. Its destruction was **archive destruction**.

**Phase 2: Destroy the Aristocracy.** Former nobles and families executed, exiled, or sent to forced labour. These families carried genealogical memory of old provincial governance. Their papers, archives, and oral traditions: eliminated.

**Phase 3: Destroy the Communes.** The *obshchina* was abolished. Collective agriculture replaced first by forced grain requisition (War Communism, 1918–1921), then catastrophic forced collectivisation under Stalin. Village communes — the last living cells of Hordian collective governance — replaced by state-managed kolkhozes with no organic connection to the communities they replaced.

**Phase 4: Construct a New History.** Soviet historians under strict Party control created the final falsification layer. Pre-1917 Russian history became a Marxist morality tale of feudalism and class struggle. The Hordian period was preserved as the humiliating "Tatar-Mongol yoke." The New Chronology perspective was made structurally impossible to discover because primary sources had been destroyed or locked away.

## The Alleged Execution of the Royal Family

On the night of 16–17 July 1918, the Romanov family was allegedly executed by a Bolshevik firing squad in the Ipatiev House basement in Yekaterinburg. The evidence is remarkably thin:

- Bodies not found until 1991 — 73 years later — only after Soviet collapse
- DNA identification contested by multiple researchers
- Original investigation (Nikolai Sokolov) conducted under chaotic Civil War conditions with mostly circumstantial evidence
- Multiple credible reports of surviving family members circulated for decades
- The Russian Orthodox Church initially refused to accept the remains

This does not prove the Romanovs survived, but establishes that the official narrative's certainty is unwarranted. What *is* certain: whether dead or alive, the dynasty that usurped the Hordian throne in 1613 ceased to exercise power in 1917.

## The Irony of the Romanov Execution

The Romanovs spent 300 years destroying every trace of the Hordian dynasty they replaced — whitewashing tomb inscriptions, relabeling graves, falsifying genealogies, burning old documents. Then they were destroyed by the same forces of historical erasure they had set in motion. The Bolsheviks applied the same logic to the Romanovs that the Romanovs had applied to the Horde.

The destroyers were destroyed. But the deeper damage — the loss of Hordian memory — was already done.
