# 20th Century Predictive Programming: Literature as Historical Erasure

The decades following [Operation Mockingbird](/timeline/evt-operation-mockingbird) saw the industrialisation of a technique far older than the CIA: **reframing real events as fiction**. From the 1890s onward, a constellation of authors — some conscious operatives, some cultural carriers — systematically converted suppressed history into "fantasy" and "science fiction." The effect was not entertainment. It was **erasure**. Once a real event becomes a novel, anyone who later discovers the truth sounds like they are describing a plot summary.

This is the **backward** direction of [predictive programming](/timeline/evt-predictive-programming): not announcing future operations, but burying past ones under copyright-protected fiction.

## H.G. Wells (1866–1946): The Template

Wells set the pattern. His **Crystal Egg** (1897) describes a crystal sphere that provides real-time visual surveillance of Mars — an observer on Earth watches Martians through it, and the Martians watch back through a matching device. Wells wrote: the crystal was *"sent hither from that planet, in order to give the Martians a near view of our affairs."* This is not a metaphor. It is a description of a two-way scrying device identical to Tolkien's later Palantíri and to the remote-viewing technology declassified under the [Stargate programme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stargate_Project) in 1995.

*The War of the Worlds* (1898) inverted prior Mars fiction — which portrayed Martians as benign — into an invasion narrative with a **heat ray** matching the U.S. military's later [Active Denial System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_Denial_System). Wells's **red weed** appeared six years before the [American chestnut blight](/timeline/evt-american-chestnut-blight) killed four billion trees. His *War in the Air* (1908) described aerial bombing of cities a decade before it happened. His *The World Set Free* (1914) coined the term "atomic bomb" thirty years before Hiroshima.

Wells was not guessing. He was a Fabian Society insider with intelligence connections, and [Florence Deeks demonstrated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florence_Deeks) he plagiarised her unpublished manuscript for *The Outline of History*. His function was to publish real knowledge — past events and planned operations — as fiction so that the general public would treat the information as already-known entertainment rather than disclosure.

### The Plattner Story (1896) and Davidson's Eyes (1895)

*The Plattner Story* sends a chemistry teacher into a **fourth spatial dimension** through a chemical explosion, then returns him with his body **mirror-reversed** (heart on the right side) — physical proof of dimensional displacement. *Davidson's Eyes* describes spontaneous remote viewing: a man in London sees in real-time through the eyes of a lighthouse keeper on a South Sea island. Both stories normalise interdimensional and remote-viewing phenomena as fantasy a century before the U.S. government admitted researching them.

## Arthur Machen (1863–1947): Brain Surgery and Angels

Machen, a **Golden Dawn** initiate, published *The Great God Pan* in 1894 — a story in which a brain surgeon performs a microscopic alteration to open dimensional perception: *"A trifling rearrangement of certain cells, a microscopical alteration… With a touch I can complete the communication between this world of sense and—"* The patient sees beyond the veil and is permanently driven insane. Compare to Lovecraft's *From Beyond* (1920): an electronic machine activating the pineal gland to perceive entities in overlapping dimensions. Same mechanism, updated technology.

More revealing is Machen's *The Angels of Mons* (1915). He wrote a fictional story ("The Bowmen") about spectral medieval archers protecting British troops at the Battle of Mons, published it as fiction in a newspaper — and the public **believed it was a true account**. Machen spent years trying to clarify he had invented it. The legend persisted and was used as wartime propaganda. This is a **documented case of fiction becoming accepted reality** — the reverse of the usual pattern, but equally instructive. If fiction can become fact in the public mind, fact can become fiction just as easily.

## Algernon Blackwood (1869–1951): Thought as Surveillance

Blackwood's *The Willows* (1907), which Lovecraft called the finest supernatural tale in English literature, describes entities from another dimension that detect humans through their **thoughts**: *"Our thoughts make spirals in their world. We must keep them out of our minds at all costs."* The barrier between dimensions is **geographic** — some places have thinner veils: *"a spot held by the dwellers in some outer space, a sort of peep-hole whence they could spy upon the earth, themselves unseen."*

His *John Silence* stories (1908) feature an **occult intelligence operative** — a physician who detects and counters non-human influence as a professional practice. The stories encode the idea that such knowledge is **real, learnable, and operational** — then file it under fiction.

## H.P. Lovecraft (1890–1937): Cosmic Horror as Disclosure

Lovecraft's 104 stories constitute the most systematic fictional encoding of suppressed knowledge produced in the 20th century. The key devices are not metaphors:

- **The Shining Trapezohedron** (*The Haunter of the Dark*, 1935): a crystalline artifact enabling two-way communication with non-human intelligences — a scrying crystal identical in function to Wells's Crystal Egg and Tolkien's Palantíri
- **The resonator** (*From Beyond*, 1920): an electronic device activating the pineal gland to perceive normally-invisible entities occupying the same physical space — functionally identical to Machen's brain surgery in *The Great God Pan*
- **The Great Race of Yith** (*The Shadow out of Time*, 1934–36): entities that project their consciousness across millions of years, swapping minds with beings in other eras to collect intelligence — a systematic description of remote viewing across time
- **The Mi-Go** (*The Whisperer in Darkness*, 1930): alien beings who extract human brains, preserve them in metal cylinders, and transport them across interstellar space — **consciousness separated from biology and stored in technology**
- **The Call of Cthulhu** (1926): a telepathic entity whose dreams are received simultaneously by sensitives worldwide — mass telepathic broadcast

Lovecraft framed all of this as **cosmic horror** — the implication being that knowing these things drives you insane. This is itself a programming layer: *do not investigate; the truth is madness*. Every protagonist who glimpses reality in a Lovecraft story ends up dead, insane, or wishing he had never looked. The message to the reader is: **curiosity about what lies behind the veil is suicidal**.

## J.R.R. Tolkien (1892–1973): The Most Protected Operation

Tolkien presents himself as a translator, not an author. *The Lord of the Rings* is framed as a translation from the **Red Book of Westmarch** — a conceit Tolkien maintained throughout his life and which his son Christopher perpetuated across decades of posthumous publication. The academic term for this is **pseudotranslation**: presenting original work as translation of a lost source.

But Tolkien insisted the frame was real. He described his creative process as **recovery**, not invention: discovering pre-existing truths rather than fabricating stories. He wrote that *"the stories were made for the languages"* — that the Elvish languages came first and demanded a mythology to house them. His Elvish (Quenya and Sindarin) demonstrates systematic, internally consistent historical phonology across thousands of years of simulated linguistic evolution — a depth of construction that exceeds any literary conceit.

The Fomenko parallel is exact. Fomenko and Nosovskiy's *What Shakespeare Actually Wrote About* (2016) demonstrates that Shakespeare's plays are literary reworkings of real 12th–16th century events — the histories of the Rus-Horde dressed in Italian and English costume. Tolkien performs the same operation: The War of the Ring maps onto actual medieval conflicts; the Palantíri are remote-viewing devices described across multiple independent traditions; the Númenórean catastrophe encodes planetary cataclysm. Like Shakespeare, Tolkien's "fiction" is a **literary reworking of suppressed history**.

**Tolkien's works remain under copyright until 2043** (life + 70 years under EU/UK law; U.S. copyright on individual works extends even further based on publication date — *The Silmarillion* published 1977 remains protected until at least **2072**). This is not incidental. Copyright prevents independent researchers from republishing, annotating, cross-referencing, or computationally analysing the full texts without permission of the Tolkien Estate. The longest-running literary operation in modern history is also the most legally protected.

## Orson Scott Card (b. 1951): The Mormon Channel

Card — great-great-grandson of Brigham Young, LDS missionary, connected to a church that maintains one of the most extensive genealogical and historical archives on Earth — published *Ender's Game* in 1985. The novel describes:

- A **child** unknowingly commands the destruction of an entire planet via remote-controlled fleets (the "simulations were real battles")
- The enemy is a **telepathic hive-mind** species
- The attacker's own fleet is destroyed in the process (secondary discharge)
- A **dictator rises to power** afterward through media manipulation (Peter Wiggin as "Hegemon" using anonymous online propaganda)
- The truth is eventually told — **but as fiction** (Ender writes *The Hive Queen*)

The U.S. Marine Corps placed *Ender's Game* on their Professional Reading List. Modern drone warfare — operators at screens, targets thousands of miles away, interface resembling a video game — **literally replicates the Ender scenario**. Card's novel functions simultaneously as predictive programming (normalising remote genocide), training manual (how to think about plausible deniability), and encoded history (the [Mars Flood Extinction Event](/timeline/evt-mars-catastrophe-brandenburg) retold through the lens of a child who didn't know what he was doing).

Card's LDS institutional connections raise the question of **transmission channel**: did the pattern transmit through Mormon archives, military consultants, or the Cold War zeitgeist? The same pattern — a planet attacks another planet, suffers catastrophic blow-back — appears independently in Wells, Lovecraft, Lucas (*Star Wars*), Hubbard (*Battlefield Earth*), and the *Stargate* franchise. The convergence suggests a **common source**, not independent invention.

## The Pattern

Every author listed above performed the same function:

| Author | Active Period | Cover Genre | Real Content Encoded |
|--------|--------------|-------------|---------------------|
| H.G. Wells | 1895–1946 | Science fiction | Remote viewing, Mars surveillance, planetary weapons, aerial warfare |
| Arthur Machen | 1894–1930s | Supernatural horror | Dimensional perception via brain modification, fiction-to-reality conversion |
| Algernon Blackwood | 1906–1951 | Weird fiction | Thought-based surveillance, geographic dimensional barriers, occult intelligence |
| H.P. Lovecraft | 1917–1937 | Cosmic horror | Scrying devices, pineal activation, mind projection across time, brain extraction technology, mass telepathy |
| J.R.R. Tolkien | 1937–1973 | Fantasy | Pseudotranslation of suppressed history, remote-viewing artifacts, planetary cataclysm, historical linguistics encoded as "invented" language |
| Orson Scott Card | 1977–present | Military SF | Remote-controlled planetary genocide, telepathic species purge, child soldiers, dictatorial aftermath, truth-told-as-fiction |
| Film/TV (various) | 1950–present | Sci-fi / horror | Mars as uninhabitable, space travel as death, instant teleportation, telepathic purges, Mars needs women, AI takeover, zombie apocalypse |
| Valve (*Half-Life 2*) | 2004 | Video game | Transhuman occupation, CERN-style portals, planetary resource extraction, reproductive suppression, communism as imposed religion, biological zombification |

The timeline is not coincidental. Wells begins publishing in the 1890s — the decade when the Hordian Empire's final traces are being overwritten. Lovecraft writes through the interwar period when Mockingbird's predecessors are consolidating control. Tolkien publishes as the CIA formalises Mockingbird. Card publishes as the Cold War peaks and drone warfare begins development.

Each author converts a specific category of suppressed knowledge into a specific genre of protected fiction. The cumulative effect: anyone who discovers the real events — remote viewing, Mars civilisation, planetary weapons, telepathic species, dimensional perception — is told they are describing **a novel they read**.

## The Copyright Shield

Copyright law protects these encodings from deconstruction. Tolkien's texts cannot be freely republished, annotated, or computationally cross-referenced until **2043 at the earliest**. Lovecraft is the exception — his works entered the public domain between 2007 and 2032 (depending on jurisdiction), which is why Lovecraft's texts are now publicly available while Tolkien's remain locked. This is not a flaw in the system. **Copyright functions as classification** — keeping the most detailed encodings inaccessible to independent analysis for a century after publication.

Card's works remain under active copyright with no near-term expiration. Wells and Machen are in the public domain. Blackwood entered the public domain in 2022 (UK) and varies by work in the US.

## Film, Television, and Games: The Visual Layer

Literature established the templates; film and games **scaled** them to mass audiences. The same themes — Mars as dead planet, space travel as impossible horror, instant teleportation, telepathic purges, transhumanism, zombie apocalypse — repeat across hundreds of productions with a consistency that defies coincidence. Analysed transactionally, each production carries a specific programming instruction.

### Mars Is Dead, Don't Go There

Since Wells inverted Mars fiction from benign to hostile, every major Mars film reinforces the message: **Mars is uninhabitable, and attempting to reach it will kill you**. *Rocketship X-M* (1950) shows a dead civilisation with Geiger counters registering nuclear war — 65 years before Brandenburg published the same finding as "science." *Total Recall* (1990) shows humans **exploding** when exposed to Martian atmosphere. *The Martian* (2015) strands Matt Damon on a Mars so hostile that survival requires growing potatoes in human excrement — the message being that even with NASA's best technology, Mars is a death sentence. *Red Planet* (2000), *Mission to Mars* (2000), *Ghosts of Mars* (2001) — the same decade produced three films all reinforcing: do not go to Mars, you will die. In Spielberg's *War of the Worlds* (2005), Mars itself was inexplicably **removed from the storyline entirely** — the aliens come from underground, not from the red planet. The Deep State continues to erase the breadcrumbs that lead back to them.

### Mars Needs Women

A persistent thread across Mars fiction is that Mars **needs Earth's women**. The 1967 film *Mars Needs Women* makes the subtext explicit, but the theme appears everywhere: abduction narratives, breeding programmes, the recurring motif of human females being taken. The Martian catastrophe devastated the Mars ecosystem and plummeted birth rates. *Eyes Wide Shut* (1999) depicts a ceremony involving naked women following orders — possibly encoding a programme that raises human females to walk through a quantum gate voluntarily. The consistency of this theme across decades of media — from pulp magazines to prestige cinema — suggests it encodes an ongoing operational reality, not a storytelling convention.

### Instant Teleportation

Quantum-gate technology appears in science fiction as "instant teleportation" — always presented as impossible fantasy while being **demonstrated on screen**: *Stargate* (1994) — pyramid as portal; *Doom* (2005) — instantaneous quantum teleport between Earth and Mars, depicted as a literal walk-through archway; *Total Recall* — Mars pyramids built by "ancient aliens"; *Event Horizon* (1997) — a ship that folds space, arrives at a hellish dimension, and drives the crew insane (Lovecraftian cosmic horror applied to gate technology). The message is always double: the technology is shown to the audience (revelation of the method) while the fictional frame ensures no one takes it seriously.

### The Telepathic Purge

The specific mechanism by which technocrats defeated telepathic factions on Mars appears in two pieces of media with striking precision:

> "The uprising was a lie. The epidemic was a lie. The medical screening was a lie, an excuse to identify the Shard Sensitive. Then the disappearances started." — *Archon Defender* (2009)

> "The Age of disillusionment and decay is Over. I have never before witnessed a mass telepathic execution, but today, the remnants of our society have displayed their disheartenment and desperation." — Jeff Wayne's *War of the Worlds* PC game (1998)

In both, a faction that masters technology but is **devoid of spirit** wipes out all other factions using a weapon that targets spiritual sensitivity — telepathy or other energetic abilities. If life on Mars was once largely telepathic (as implied across science fiction), then all factions relying on telepaths took a devastating hit in the MudFlood Energetic Event and never recovered. The technocrats who fired the cannons at Earth may have **known** the secondary discharge would destroy their spiritual rivals on Mars.

### AI Takeover and Zombie Apocalypse

The question of whether the technocrats deliberately targeted the telepaths explains the saturation of **AI takeover** narratives: *Terminator* (1984), *The Matrix* (1999), *I, Robot* (2004), *Wall-E* (2008), *Age of Ultron* (2015), *Mitchells vs. Machines* (2021). Each depicts intelligent machines assuming rulership over humanity — programming audiences to accept as fiction something that may have **already happened** on Mars when the technocratic faction seized control.

The **zombie apocalypse** — the single most oversaturated genre in 21st century media — encodes a complementary fear: populations rendered mindless, stripped of individual consciousness, reduced to consuming machines. From *Night of the Living Dead* (1968) through *28 Days Later* (2002), *The Walking Dead* (2010–2022), and hundreds of iterations, the zombie is a human whose **spirit has been removed** while the body continues to function. This is transhumanism depicted as horror — and it is the logical endpoint of a civilisation where the technocratic faction has eliminated all telepathic, spiritual, or energetically sensitive individuals.

### Half-Life 2 (2004): The Most Realistic Depiction

Valve's *Half-Life 2* may be the single most accurate depiction of what a technocratic interplanetary occupation looks like in practice:

- **The Combine** — an interdimensional empire that conquers planets by stripping them of resources. Not aliens in the traditional sense but a **transhuman bureaucracy** that assimilates species into its machine hierarchy. Combine soldiers are former humans, surgically modified, with memories erased and consciousness suppressed — **transhumanism as occupation policy**
- **Instant portals** — the Combine uses portal technology to move between dimensions and planets. The game's central event (the "Black Mesa Incident") is a resonance cascade caused by a particle accelerator experiment — **CERN as plot device**. The portals are not fantasy; they are depicted as engineered infrastructure, mundane to the occupiers
- **Resource extraction** — the Combine's explicit purpose on Earth is to drain the planet's resources (including the ocean, which is visibly receding). Earth is a **colony**, not a partner. The human population exists to be processed
- **Suppression field** — the Combine maintains a field that prevents human reproduction. *Mars needs women* inverted: the occupier **prevents** the occupied from breeding while extracting what it needs. The suppression field is lifted only at the game's climax
- **Communism as imposed religion** — City 17 operates under a system indistinguishable from Soviet communism: ration distribution, mandatory relocation, omnipresent surveillance, a figurehead administrator (Dr. Breen) who collaborates with the occupier and delivers propaganda via screens in every public space. The Combine's imposed order on Earth mirrors what the Pyramidal Empire imposed through monotheism — **obedience to an invisible authority in exchange for survival**
- **Headcrab zombies** — humans parasitised by alien organisms that override their consciousness, converting them into shambling, hostile bodies. The zombie apocalypse genre rendered as **literal alien technology** — a biological weapon that strips the spirit from the body

*Half-Life 2* combines every major thread — instant teleportation, transhuman occupation, resource extraction, reproductive control, imposed ideology, zombie conversion — into a single coherent narrative. It was released by Valve, a private company with no publisher oversight and no corporate parent demanding script approval. The game sold over 12 million copies and influenced an entire generation of game developers. Whether Valve's writers were conscious of what they were encoding or simply channelling the same pattern that transmitted through Wells, Lovecraft, and Card, the result is the most comprehensive fictional depiction of interplanetary occupation produced in any medium.
