# Mars Literature as Reclassified Nonfiction

Between 1880 and 1938, over a dozen authors across five countries independently described the same Mars: inhabited by humans, governed by hidden polar hierarchies, connected to Earth through surveillance and blood. Then their books were reclassified as "science fiction" — a [genre that did not exist](/timeline/evt-controlled-opposition) when most were written.

**The thesis:** Pre-Wells consensus (Schiaparelli, Flammarion, Lowell) agreed Mars was inhabited. Fiction reflected the science. Wells's *War of the Worlds* (1898) **inverted** this: benign human Martians became hostile monsters. His template became the default. The pulp tradition (Burroughs, Bogdanov, Lewis, Bradbury) preserved the older consensus. Gernsback's "scientifiction" (1926) created a quarantine genre. NASA (1960s+) finalised: Mars is dead. Three centuries of testimony buried under a label — not because someone proved Mars uninhabited, but because someone invented a genre and filed them in it.

**Four phases:** (1) Pre-1898 open consensus; (2) 1898 Wells inversion; (3) 1926–50 genre creation and quarantine; (4) 1960s+ institutional authority.
