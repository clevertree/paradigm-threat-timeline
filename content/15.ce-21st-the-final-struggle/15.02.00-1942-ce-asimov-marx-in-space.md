# 1942–1992 CE — Asimov: Marx in Space!

Isaac Asimov (1920–1992), born in Soviet Russia, became one of the "Big Three" SF writers. His work belongs in the [predictive programming](/timeline/evt-predictive-programming) tradition with [Wells, Lovecraft, Tolkien, Card](/timeline/evt-20th-century-predictive-programming-literature).

**The thesis:** The Three Laws of Robotics are not for robots — they are **slave codes**. OT golem doctrine (soulless, below animals) gave theological warrant; Marxism/colonialism gave the social template. Asimov did not realise he was encoding slave commandments. Foundation = Marx in space: empire collapse, class analysis, religion as control, psychohistory as materialist determinism. His robots occupy the same structural position as enslaved persons. If robots exist or existed on Mars, Asimov may have received second-hand knowledge via Binder brothers/pulp and reframed it as invention.
