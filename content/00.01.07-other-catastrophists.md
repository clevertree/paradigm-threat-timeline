# Other Catastrophists and Chronology Revisionists

The following researchers challenged mainstream chronology, cosmology, or human prehistory in ways directly relevant to this investigation. Each is listed by their primary contribution. Those with dedicated articles elsewhere in the timeline are excluded here.

## Immanuel Velikovsky (1895–1979)

**Primary contribution:** Catastrophist reconstruction of ancient history through comparative mythology and ancient texts.

Velikovsky was a Russian-Jewish psychiatrist and independent scholar whose 1950 book *Worlds in Collision* argued that Venus was ejected from Jupiter as a comet within human memory, made multiple near-passes to Earth causing the plagues of Exodus and the standing still of the sun for Joshua, and eventually settled into its present orbit. His 1952 *Ages in Chaos* followed with a chronological revision of Egyptian history, arguing that Egyptian records were mismatched with biblical history by approximately 500 years.

Velikovsky's central claims:
- **Venus was a comet** within the last 3,500 years, not a primordial planet
- **Planetary catastrophism happened during recorded history**, not geological deep time
- **Ancient myths are literal eyewitness records** of celestial events
- **Egyptian chronology is inflated by ~500 years**, creating a false synchronization gap with Hebrew history

His work was suppressed with organized ferocity: an academic boycott of his publisher Macmillan was organized in 1950, forcing the book to be transferred to Doubleday. Carl Sagan led a late-career campaign against Velikovsky at the 1974 AAAS conference, which Fomenko and others have noted reads more like a political trial than scientific inquiry. Velikovsky's methods — comparative mythology, textual analysis, and astronomical inference — were the direct inspiration for Wal Thornhill, the Thunderbolts Project, and the Saturn myth researchers.

> *"Fomenko notes that Velikovsky was essentially a defender of the Scaligerian version of modern history, only shifting ancient history. He is considered a founder of the 'critical school' in chronology, but his more conservative revisions may have inhibited the development of deeper New Chronology work in the West."*
> — [History of the New Chronology](https://chronologia.org/en/seven/history.html), chronologia.org

**Key works:** *Worlds in Collision* (1950), *Ages in Chaos* (1952), *Peoples of the Sea* (1977), *Ramses II and His Time* (1978)

## Anatoly Fomenko (b. 1945)

**Primary contribution:** Mathematical and statistical proof that Scaligerian chronology is a fabrication.

Anatoly Timofeevich Fomenko is a Russian mathematician, professor at Moscow State University, member of the Russian Academy of Sciences, and a leading topologist. Beginning in 1973, he turned statistical analysis toward published historical records — eventually analyzing over 2,200 sources — and discovered what he characterized as systematic misdating.

His key findings:
- **Three chronological shifts** of approximately 333, 1053, and 1800 years are embedded in Scaligerian chronology — the same brief chronicle copied four times and presented as distinct eras
- **Lunar acceleration anomalies** (building on Robert Newton's 1972 work) show that ancient eclipse records are chronologically misplaced
- **All "ancient" civilization is medieval** — Greek, Roman, and Egyptian records all describe events no earlier than approximately 900 CE, not BCE
- **Andronicus-Christ** was born 25 December 1152 CE in the Crimea and crucified outside Constantinople in 1185 CE — not in 1st-century Palestine
- **The Mongol Empire and the Russian-Horde are the same entity** — "Genghis Khan" is a title, not a personal name

Fomenko's *New Chronology* (7 volumes) was published beginning in the 1990s. The work received no mainstream academic acknowledgment despite its mathematical rigor. It is the primary chronological framework for this timeline's historical section.

**Key works:** *History: Fiction or Science?* (Vols. 1–7, 1999–2006), *New Chronology of Russia* (1995), *New Chronology of Greece and Bible* (1996)

## David Talbott (b. 1942)

**Primary contribution:** The Saturn Myth — reconstruction of the Golden Age polar configuration from comparative mythology.

David Talbott is an independent researcher and co-founder of the Thunderbolts Project. His 1980 book *The Saturn Myth* argued — on the basis of a worldwide corpus of ancient mythological imagery — that Saturn once occupied a fixed position at Earth's polar axis, appearing as a vastly enlarged stationary "sun" before a catastrophic reconfiguration dispersed the configuration. He identified consistent cross-cultural archetypes (the "Wheel of Heaven," the "Cosmic Mountain," the "World Column," the "Ship of Heaven") as independent records of the same sky.

Talbott's central contributions:
- **The polar configuration** — Saturn, Venus, and Mars were arrayed along a common polar axis visible from Earth's northern hemisphere in the Golden Age
- **The "Wheel of Heaven"** archetype appears in Babylonian, Egyptian, Hindu, Norse, Maya, and Aztec sources as a direct visual record of Saturn's plasma corona and spokes
- **The Great Catastrophe** — the breakup of the polar configuration was the historical event underlying flood myths, fire-from-heaven myths, and the "death of the gods" across all cultures
- **Mythological convergence** is the primary evidentiary method: if 30+ unconnected ancient cultures describe the same impossible sky, it was observed, not invented

His collaboration with Wal Thornhill produced the theoretical bridge between the mythological reconstruction and the EU physics framework.

**Key works:** *The Saturn Myth* (1980), *Thunderbolts of the Gods* (2005, with Wal Thornhill), *The Electric Universe* (2007, with Wal Thornhill)

## Anthony Peratt (b. 1940)

**Primary contribution:** Laboratory plasma physics confirmation of the ancient polar aurora as a Z-pinch event recorded in petroglyphs worldwide.

Anthony Peratt is a plasma physicist from Los Alamos National Laboratory and a student of Hannes Alfvén. His research began in plasma physics but shifted to archaeoastronomy after he noticed that ancient petroglyphs worldwide depicted patterns identical to those produced in laboratory high-current Z-pinch plasma discharge experiments.

His central contribution:
- **Petroglyphs as eyewitness records** — over 100 distinct petroglyph types found on every inhabited continent map precisely to specific phases of Z-pinch plasma discharge, including the "squatting man" (stickman), columnar stacks, dot-in-circle, and concentric ring motifs
- **The 3 southern plasmoids** — Peratt's 2003 paper identified three plasma columns in the ancient southern sky corresponding to Mercury, Neptune, and Uranus in the Saturnian lower configuration
- **Quantitative dating** — the plasma intensities required to produce the observed petroglyph patterns provide a physical constraint on when the event occurred
- **Location geometry** — the angle of incidence of petroglyph imagery relative to the horizon matches predictions for specific latitudes, confirming a common source at the celestial south pole

> *"Part I deals with the comparison of graphical and radiation data from high current Z-pinches to petroglyphs, geo-glyphs, and megaliths. Part II focuses on the source of light and its temporal change from a current-increasing Z-pinch or dense-plasma-focus aurora."*
> — [Characteristics for the Occurrence of a High Current Z-Pinch Aurora as Recorded in Antiquity](https://www.academia.edu/9156691)

**Key works:** *Characteristics for the Occurrence of a High Current Z-Pinch Aurora as Recorded in Antiquity* (Parts I & II, 2003)

## Heribert Illig (b. 1947)

**Primary contribution:** The Phantom Time Hypothesis — 297 fabricated years in the early medieval calendar.

Heribert Illig is a German author and publisher whose 1991 book *Das erfundene Mittelalter* (*The Invented Middle Ages*) proposed that the years 614–911 CE were inserted into the historical record as a fabrication by Holy Roman Emperor Otto III, Pope Sylvester II, and Byzantine Emperor Constantine VII — collaborating around the symbolic year 1000 CE to place their reign at the millennium.

The core claims:
- **No archaeological, astronomical, or architectural evidence** survives that unambiguously dates to 614–911 CE
- **Carolingian architecture**, including structures attributed to Charlemagne, shows stylistic inconsistencies with its claimed period and was fabricated or misattributed
- **The Julian calendar** accumulated a 13-day error by 1582 that should correspond to 1,200+ years of uncorrected drift, but only ~1,000 years had elapsed — the 300-year gap exactly matches the phantom period
- **Charlemagne himself** may be a largely constructed figure inserted into the phantom era

Illig's work overlaps with Fomenko's New Chronology at the broad structural level (both identify a fabricated early medieval gap) but reaches the conclusion through different methodological paths and with a different proposed mechanism.

**Key works:** *Das erfundene Mittelalter* (1991), *Wer hat an der Uhr gedreht?* (1999)

## Gunnar Heinsohn (b. 1943)

**Primary contribution:** Stratigraphic chronology — archaeological strata do not support the conventional ancient timeline.

Gunnar Heinsohn is a German sociologist and independent historian at the University of Bremen. He argued from archaeological stratigraphy that the conventional ancient timeline is chronologically inflated — the same physical strata are being assigned to multiple eras (Assyrian, Babylonian, Persian, Hellenistic, Roman, early medieval) that are actually contemporaneous or sequential within a much shorter span.

His specific revisions:
- **The 1st millennium BCE "ghost empires"** — Assyrian, Neo-Babylonian, and Achaemenid Persian empires do not occupy distinct stratigraphic layers; they are phantom reconstructions of the same stratum
- **Roman and early medieval are the same period** — "Late Roman" and the "Migration Period" are not centuries apart but are the same catastrophic event seen from different editorial perspectives
- **The 10th century CE catastrophe** — Heinsohn identified a global stratigraphic discontinuity around 930–940 CE corresponding to a major cosmic event that ended the Roman world and reset human settlement patterns
- His work directly challenges the standard 3,000-year span of "ancient history" and supports a compressed medieval-only timeline consistent with Fomenko

**Key works:** *Who are the Sumerians?* (1988), numerous papers at q-mag.org and academia.edu

## Dwardu Cardona (1937–2018)

**Primary contribution:** The most detailed mythological reconstruction of the Saturnian polar configuration, particularly the pre-configuration "God Star" era.

Dwardu Cardona was a Maltese-Canadian independent researcher who spent decades developing the mytho-historical evidence for the Saturnian polar configuration. He worked closely with David Talbott and contributed extensively to the journal *Kronos* and later *AEON*.

His contributions:
- **The God Star series** — six volumes reconstructing the sequence of Saturnian events from Saturn as single "sun" through the polar configuration to the catastrophic dispersal
- **Linguistic analysis** — traced the name "Saturn" and cognates across dozens of ancient cultures pointing to a single referent star/body
- **The proto-Saturnian epoch** — argued Saturn shone as a lone "sun" before entering the solar system and capturing Earth, meaning the earliest mythological memories predate the polar configuration itself
- **Detailed sequencing** of the mythological events in the polar configuration era, adding precision to Talbott's framework

**Key works:** *God Star* (2006), *Flare Star* (2007), *Primordial Star* (2009), *Birth of the Gods* (2010), *Vishnu Born of the Sun* (2014), *Saturn-Myth Revisited* (2018)

## Ev Cochrane (b. 1952)

**Primary contribution:** Systematic mythology analysis of Mars and Venus as catastrophist celestial actors.

Ev Cochrane is an independent scholar and editor of the journal *AEON* who applied comparative mythology methodology to establish that ancient myths about Mars and Venus describe a real sequence of catastrophic close-pass events witnessed from Earth.

His central work:
- **Venus as plasmoid** — documented the cross-cultural universal depiction of Venus as a multi-armed, hairy, star-like warrior goddess (*Inanna*, *Ishtar*, *Athena*, *Aphrodite*, *Quetzalcoatl*) consistent with a plasma-discharge column rather than a point of light
- **Mars as the cosmic warrior** — argued that Mars myths (the warrior god, the dragon-slayer, the descending hero) recorded real near-Earth passes of Mars within the polar configuration
- **The "comet Venus" tradition** across Mesoamerican, Sumerian, Greek, and Hindu sources independently corroborates Velikovsky's comet-Venus claim
- **Mythology as primary data** — his methodological argument that mythology cannot be explained by psychological projection or nature allegory alone; it is observational record

**Key works:** *The Many Faces of Venus* (1997), *Martian Metamorphoses: The Planet Mars in Ancient Myth and Religion* (1997), *Stairway to Heaven* (1999)

## Victor Clube & Bill Napier

**Primary contribution:** Mainstream astronomical confirmation of catastrophic comet-swarm events within recorded human history.

Victor Clube (Oxford) and Bill Napier (Armagh Observatory) were British astronomers who argued within the mainstream scientific framework that a large comet entered the inner solar system within the last 20,000 years, fragmented, and produced a debris stream that bombarded Earth in multiple episodes throughout human prehistory and history.

Their framework:
- **The Taurid Complex** — the present-day Taurid meteor stream is the remnant of a giant comet estimated at 50–100 km diameter that entered the solar system ~20,000–30,000 years ago and fragmented progressively
- **Coherent catastrophism** — periodic encounters with the Taurid debris stream would produce catastrophic bombardment events on a cycle of centuries to millennia, not the random-impact model of standard geology
- **Historical catastrophe within civilization** — they argued that the Bronze Age Collapse (~1200 BCE), the Roman Dark Age, and the early medieval dark horizon are all bombardment events, not social collapse
- **Sky religion as bombardment memory** — dragon myths, sky-serpent traditions, and comet-worship across cultures are records of actual cometary apparitions, not pure myth

Their work is notable because it reached catastrophist conclusions from within conventional astronomy, making it harder to dismiss on institutional grounds.

**Key works:** *The Cosmic Serpent* (1982), *The Cosmic Winter* (1990)

## Alfred de Grazia (1919–2014)

**Primary contribution:** "Quantavolution" — the theoretical framework for rapid, catastrophic change as the driver of geological and human history.

Alfred de Grazia was an American political scientist and independent scholar who coined the term *quantavolution* to describe rapid, step-wise changes in Earth's geology, biology, and human society driven by cosmic catastrophe. He was a close associate of Velikovsky and a principal systematizer of catastrophist theory.

His contributions:
- **Quantavolution framework** — explicitly opposed uniformitarianism (gradual change over vast time) and proposed that all major geological and biological transitions occurred in sudden catastrophic pulses
- **God's Fire** (1983) — argued that the Exodus events described real electromagnetic and plasma phenomena accompanying Venus's near-pass of Earth, including electrical discharges, plasma tornadoes, and atmospheric ionization events
- **The Lately Tortured Earth** (1983) — geological argument that Earth's surface features are better explained by recent catastrophic events than by slow erosion and plate tectonics
- **The Burning of Troy** — argued prehistoric Greek mythology records the same catastrophic period as the Exodus, with Troy destroyed by electromagnetic catastrophe rather than human siege
- Published and edited the journal *Kronos* (with Lynn Rose and Livio Stecchini), which was the primary venue for catastrophist scholarship in the 1970s and 1980s

**Key works:** *The Lately Tortured Earth* (1983), *God's Fire* (1983), *The Burning of Troy* (1984), *The Cosmic Heretics* (1984)

## Tom Van Flandern (1940–2009)

**Primary contribution:** The Exploded Planet Hypothesis — the asteroid belt and comets originate from a planet that exploded within the solar system.

Tom Van Flandern was an astronomer at the United States Naval Observatory who argued that the asteroid belt is not leftover planetesimal material from solar system formation but is instead the debris of a former full-sized planet that exploded approximately 65 million years ago.

His claims:
- **Asteroid belt composition** — the asteroids have too many distinct mineralogical types for a single-source coalescence model; they are consistent with the exploded core, mantle, and crust of a differentiated planet
- **Short-period comets** — cannot have survived 4.5 billion years in the Oort Cloud; they originate from the same explosion event and are far younger than the solar system
- **Mars is a former moon** of the exploded planet, captured by the Sun after the explosion — explaining Mars's asymmetric cratering, its outsized satellites Phobos and Deimos (which have too-rapid orbital decay for captured objects), and its trace atmosphere
- **The K-Pg boundary** — the extinction event attributed to a Chicxulub impactor may be the leading edge of debris from the planetary explosion

Van Flandern's work has direct relevance to this timeline's treatment of Mars, which in the Saturnian model is described as a hollow former planet that participated in the polar configuration.

**Key works:** *Dark Matter, Missing Planets and New Comets* (1993)

## Robert Schoch (b. 1959)

**Primary contribution:** Geological re-dating of the Great Sphinx to at least 7,000–10,000 BCE based on water erosion analysis.

Robert Schoch is an associate professor of natural science at Boston University who specializes in stratigraphy and geomorphology. In 1991 he announced that the Great Sphinx of Giza shows water erosion patterns consistent with prolonged heavy rainfall — a climate that last existed in Egypt no earlier than 7,000 BCE and possibly as early as 10,000 BCE, three to five times the conventional date.

His contributions:
- **The Sphinx water erosion** — the vertical undulating erosion channels in the Sphinx enclosure are consistent with thousands of years of heavy rainfall, not wind erosion or the brief rains of the last 4,000 years. This would push the Sphinx's construction into the pre-Dynastic or even pre-agricultural era
- **Solar outburst hypothesis** — in his later work (*Forgotten Civilization*, 2012) Schoch argued that a massive solar plasma outburst around 9,700–9,600 BCE ended the last ice age catastrophically and destroyed an earlier advanced civilization, correlating with Plato's date for Atlantis
- **Plasma discharge and ancient monuments** — connected ancient lightning bolt art, petroglyphs, and megalithic site destruction to the solar plasma event

Schoch's geological work is significant because it comes from within academic geology and cannot be dismissed as UFO speculation. His later plasma work aligns directly with the EU catastrophist framework.

**Key works:** *Voices of the Rocks* (1999), *Forgotten Civilization* (2012)

## Graham Hancock (b. 1950)

**Primary contribution:** Evidence for a lost advanced civilization destroyed by the Younger Dryas impact event (~10,900 BCE).

Graham Hancock is a British journalist and author who has spent decades arguing that an advanced prehistoric civilization was destroyed by the Younger Dryas cosmic impact event and that its survivors seeded the "sudden" emergence of agriculture and monumental architecture across the ancient world.

His contributions to this investigation:
- **Göbekli Tepe and precursor civilizations** — the construction of Göbekli Tepe (~9600 BCE) requires a civilizational substrate that conventional prehistory does not account for; Hancock argues this substrate was wiped out by the Younger Dryas event
- **The Younger Dryas Impact Hypothesis** — the sudden onset of the Younger Dryas cold period (~10,900 BCE) was caused by a fragmenting comet, consistent with the Taurid Complex model, producing global fires, the extinction of megafauna, and the destruction of coastal civilizations
- **Fingerprints of the Gods** methodology — used astronomical alignments, precessional cycles, and monument orientations to argue that ancient sites encode knowledge of a pre-catastrophe civilization
- **Underwater archaeology** — documented submerged structures off India, Japan, and the Mediterranean that are inconsistent with the conventional human prehistory timeline, suggesting coastal civilizations before the post-glacial sea-level rise

**Key works:** *Fingerprints of the Gods* (1995), *Heaven's Mirror* (1998), *Underworld* (2002), *Magicians of the Gods* (2015), *America Before* (2019)

## Michael Cremo (b. 1948)

**Primary contribution:** *Forbidden Archaeology* — systematic documentation of suppressed evidence for a far greater antiquity of the human species.

Michael Cremo (Drutakarma Dasa) is an independent researcher and member of the Bhaktivedanta Institute who, with co-author Richard Thompson, produced the 1993 volume *Forbidden Archaeology* — a 900-page compilation of archaeological and anthropological findings that place anatomically modern humans far earlier than the accepted Out-of-Africa chronology allows.

His contributions:
- **Suppressed anatomical findings** — documented hundreds of cases where modern human skeletal remains or artifacts were found in geological strata dated to millions of years ago, cases that were quietly discarded by the mainstream literature rather than addressed
- **The knowledge filter** — argued that scientific institutions systematically exclude evidence that contradicts the established human evolution timeline, essentially applying non-scientific criteria to what counts as "real" data
- **Vedic antiquity** — connected the archaeological evidence to Vedic cosmological accounts of cyclical human civilizations stretching back billions of years
- **A case for extreme human antiquity** — if even a fraction of the suppressed cases are valid, the human species is far older than the 200,000-year consensus, with implications for every chronological framework in this timeline

**Key works:** *Forbidden Archaeology* (1993, with Richard L. Thompson), *The Hidden History of the Human Race* (1994), *Human Devolution* (2003)
