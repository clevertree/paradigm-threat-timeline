# Before Creation

![Before Creation](/media/01.before-creation/before-creation.png)

Ancient testimony from different civilizations agrees that there was no *history* (or time) before the "creation" event. In all accounts, the time before the Golden Age was a lasting darkness. During this period, Saturn's dwarf system was receiving its first energy from the Sun, and with this energy came the first life forms capable of memory. As Saturn had not yet joined the Sun's orbit, there was no way to account for time.

## Saturn as a Brown Dwarf Star

According to Jno Cook's *Recovering the Lost World* ([Chapter 2: A Synopsis](https://saturniancosmology.org/syn.php)), Earth was not always a planet of our Sun. From genesis, Earth was a satellite of Saturn — then a **sub-brown dwarf star** wandering through space. Saturn generated faint infrared light and plasma output, sustaining the Saturnian System far from solar influence. The gas planets Venus, Neptune, Uranus, and Jupiter were not yet formed, nor were Mercury or Mars. All planets would reach their final form in the electronic bond forming between our Sun and Brown Dwarf Saturn.

## The Long Darkness: A Universal Memory

Cross-cultural mythology from every continent converges on one memory: before "creation," the sky was formless, dark, and chaotic. As Cook writes in [Chapter 13: The Creation](https://saturniancosmology.org/polar.php):

> *"It was universally held that the past before 'creation' was a long period during which the skies were in chaos and featureless turmoil."*

This matches sources from Egypt, Mesopotamia, Mesoamerica, India, Scandinavia, and indigenous traditions worldwide. The myths describe a real observational condition: Earth was enveloped inside Saturn's **plasma coma** in **glow mode**, obscuring all stars and the Sun. From Earth's surface, nothing was visible but swirling luminous mist.

Dwardu Cardona's *God Star* (2006) traces these "chaos" descriptions across mythological traditions, noting the primordial condition was typically described as a hovering cloud or ocean of mist, often with a twisted column of vapor reaching from sky to Earth — consistent with a plasma discharge in glow mode.

## The Egg of Creation: ~4900 BC

Around **4900 BC**, after thousands of years of featureless sky, a circular shape became dimly visible through swirling clouds above the North Pole — a globe ten to twenty times the Moon's apparent diameter. This was Saturn, its glow-mode coma beginning to thin. This is the origin of the **Cosmic Egg** motif found globally:

- Egyptian creator God Ra emerging from the primeval egg
- Vedic creator Brahma, whose sacred animal is the *goose* (Uranus displaced from the central axis, appearing like a duck's head with neck connected to Saturn's globe)
- Orphic and Phoenician creation eggs
- Greek *Ouranus* ("Father Sky") as the original globe overhead

The Egyptian hieroglyph for Ra is a duck. Egg-shaped designs appear on Eastern European pottery from approximately 5000 BC — precisely when Cook dates the Egg's first visibility.

## Why There Was No Time Before Creation

Without a visible Sun, there could be no solar calendar — no days, seasons, or years. The only observable motion was Saturn's daily rotation about the north polar axis.

Cook notes in [Chapter 14: The Start of Time](https://saturniancosmology.org/time.php) that when Saturn **nova'd** in **4077 BC** — transitioning from glow mode to arc mode and blazing suddenly like a sun — Earth's skies cleared for the first time. The Sun became visible. *Regular solar time* began. All founding myths, king lists, and calendrical "year one" dates cluster at or after this event. This is "Creation."
