# 5000 BCE — Before Creation

![Before Creation](/media/01.before-creation/before-creation.png)

Ancient testimony from every civilisation agrees: there was no history — no *time* — before "creation." All accounts describe the pre–Golden Age as lasting darkness. Saturn's dwarf system was receiving its first energy from the Sun, and with that energy came the first life forms capable of memory. As Saturn had not yet joined the Sun's orbit, there was no mechanism for reckoning time.

## Saturn as a Brown Dwarf Star

According to Jno Cook's *Recovering the Lost World* ([Chapter 2: A Synopsis](https://saturniancosmology.org/syn.php)), Earth was not always a planet of our Sun. From genesis, Earth was a satellite of Saturn — then a **sub-brown dwarf star** wandering through space. Saturn generated faint infrared light and plasma output, sustaining the Saturnian System far from solar influence. Venus, Neptune, Uranus, and Jupiter were not yet formed, nor were Mercury or Mars. All planets reached their final form through the electronic bond forming between our Sun and Brown Dwarf Saturn.

## The Long Darkness: A Universal Memory

Cross-cultural mythology from every continent converges on one memory: before "creation," the sky was formless, dark, and chaotic. As Cook writes in [Chapter 13: The Creation](https://saturniancosmology.org/polar.php):

> *"It was universally held that the past before 'creation' was a long period during which the skies were in chaos and featureless turmoil."*

Egypt, Mesopotamia, Mesoamerica, India, Scandinavia, and indigenous traditions worldwide corroborate this. The myths describe a real observational condition: Earth was enveloped inside Saturn's **plasma coma** in **glow mode**, obscuring all stars and the Sun. From the surface, nothing was visible but swirling luminous mist.

Dwardu Cardona's *God Star* (2006) traces these "chaos" descriptions across mythological traditions, noting the primordial condition was typically described as a hovering cloud or ocean of mist with a twisted vapour column reaching sky to Earth — consistent with plasma discharge in glow mode.

## The Egg of Creation: ~4900 BC

Around **4900 BC**, after millennia of featureless sky, a circular shape became dimly visible through swirling clouds above the North Pole — a globe ten to twenty times the Moon's apparent diameter. This was Saturn, its glow-mode coma beginning to thin. This is the **Cosmic Egg** motif found globally:

- Egyptian creator god Ra emerging from the primeval egg
- Vedic Brahma, whose sacred animal is the *goose* (Uranus displaced from the central axis, appearing like a duck's head and neck connected to Saturn's globe)
- Orphic and Phoenician creation eggs
- Greek *Ouranus* ("Father Sky") as the original globe overhead

The Egyptian hieroglyph for Ra is a duck. Egg-shaped designs on Eastern European pottery from approximately 5000 BC match Cook's date for the Egg's first visibility.

## Why There Was No Time Before Creation

Without a visible Sun there could be no solar calendar — no days, seasons, or years. The only observable motion was Saturn's daily rotation about the north polar axis.

Cook notes in [Chapter 14: The Start of Time](https://saturniancosmology.org/time.php) that when Saturn **nova'd** in **4077 BC** — transitioning from glow mode to arc mode and blazing suddenly like a sun — Earth's skies cleared for the first time. The Sun became visible. *Regular solar time* began. All founding myths, king lists, and calendrical "year one" dates cluster at or after this event. This is "Creation."
