# The Seven Days of Creation: A Compressed Memory of the Saturnian Epoch

The Genesis creation account is **literally true** — but compressed. Each "day" is a cosmological epoch lasting hundreds of years, bookended by planetary climaxes. The internal contradiction — light exists on Days 1–3 but the Sun is not created until Day 4 — dissolves once you recognise that time was reckoned by **Kronos** (Saturn), not our Sun.

**Key dates:** Day 1 = 4077 BC (Saturn arc-mode ignition); Day 7 = 3147 BC (Golden Age collapse, Saturn displaced). The seven-day week encodes this sequence; Saturday is Saturn's day because Saturn's displacement was the defining end-point of creation.
