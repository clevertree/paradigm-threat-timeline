## Plato's Description Cannot Be Located on Earth

The only ancient account of Atlantis comes from Plato's *Timaeus* and *Critias* (c. 360 BCE). Read literally, his description is geographically impossible for present Earth — but consistent with a city at the **north pole of Mars**, elevated above Earth in the collinear configuration.

**"Larger than Libya and Asia combined"** (*Timaeus* 24e–25a). No landmass satisfies this. The ocean floor has been mapped — no such continent exists. In the Saturnian framework, **Mars** during the Golden Age was nearly as large as Earth, accessible from Earth's north pole via the plasma column. The "Pillars of Heracles" mark the terrestrial end of the quantum bridge; "other islands" are intermediate plasma bodies; the "whole continent" is Mars.

**"Ships and voyages were not yet"** (*Critias* 113e–114a). If Atlantis couldn't be reached by ship, it wasn't mid-Atlantic. Plato describes a place reached by the **Bifröst** — the natural plasma bridge along the planetary column's magnetic axis.

**Concentric rings "sheltered from the north"** (*Critias* 113d–116a, 118b–c). A city around a north pole with the continent falling away southward matches the **Martian north polar cap** exactly. Orichalcum, the mysterious metal "dug out in many parts of the island," has never been identified on Earth — consistent with Martian surface mineralogy.

**Repeated catastrophes** (*Critias* 111–112). Egyptian priests confirmed cyclical destruction: Atlantis was not a sunken island but a peer civilization destroyed during Saturn-system collapse (c. 3147 BCE) and the Flood accompanying Mars' orbital departure.
