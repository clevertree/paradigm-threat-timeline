# Shakespeare Encodes the Great Empire

![Shakespeare Encodes the Great Empire](/media/11.ce-17th-romanovs-rise-to-power/shakespeare.png)

William Shakespeare (1564–1616) lived precisely when the Great Empire was fragmenting and its history was being rewritten. In [SAK], Fomenko and Nosovskiy demonstrate that Shakespeare's major plays are **encoded accounts of real events from the XII–XVI centuries**, set in the metropoly of the Great Empire. Their "ancient" settings are a product of the same chronological displacement that affected all European historiography.

## The Identifications

From [Chapter 7, §33](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/07_30.html):

> *"In the book [SAK] we show that such extraordinary Shakespearian plays as 'Hamlet', 'King Lear', 'Macbeth', 'Timon of Athens', 'Henry VIII', 'Titus Andronicus' in fact give an account of real and important events. Of the XII–XVI cc. unfolding mainly in the metropoly of the Great Empire."*

| **Macbeth** | The Biblical King Herod | XII |

The character of Emperor Andronicus-Christ appears across multiple plays: Prince Hamlet, MacDuff, philosopher Apemantus, and Titus Andronicus.

## The Method

Fomenko and Nosovskiy cross-referenced Shakespeare's plots with the chronicles of **Geoffrey of Monmouth**, **Saxo Grammaticus**, and **Raphael Holinshed** — Shakespeare's known sources, who themselves wrote about the Great Empire under displaced names and dates. When NC chronological corrections are applied to all sources simultaneously, the same sequence of events — coup, betrayal, massacre, exile — appears in the drama, the chronicle, and the biblical text.

> *"A dramatist and a poet add to the ancient chronicle some made-up details and emotionally decorate a scanty plot. The literary emotions take centre stage and conceal the true essence."*

## The XII Century Plays: Christ and His Epoch

- **Hamlet**: Father murdered, prince seeks justice — the betrayal and crucifixion of Christ (= Andrei Bogolyubsky), with the Danish court standing in for Czar-Grad
- **Macbeth**: Herod's massacre of the innocents encoded as Scottish succession crisis; MacDuff = Christ survives the slaughter
- **Timon of Athens**: Generous benefactor betrayed — Judas Iscariot's betrayal inverted as a parable about ingratitude
- **Titus Andronicus**: The name itself preserves the identification

## The XVI Century Plays: Ivan the Terrible and the Oprichnina

- **King Lear**: Aging king divides his realm, destroyed by ungrateful children — Ivan the Terrible's division of the Empire and civil war. Cordelia = the Horde-loyalist faction.
- **Henry VIII**: English marital dramas encode the Russian succession crisis. Catherine of Aragon = Sophia Palaiologina; Anne Boleyn = Elena Voloshanka = [Biblical Esther](/content/10.ce-16th-reformation-and-inquisition/10.06.01-redacted-into-the-books-of-esther-judith.md)

Shakespeare was writing about events one or two generations past — not ancient history. The displacement to "ancient Denmark," "Scotland," "Rome" is the same obfuscation applied to all European historiography in the XVII–XVIII centuries.

## One or More Authors?

The NC analysis does not require resolving the authorship question. What matters is that the **source material** — the chronicles — encoded Great Empire history. However, the *specificity* of the parallels implies the playwright(s) had access to suppressed Hordian chronicles being destroyed in the early XVII century.
