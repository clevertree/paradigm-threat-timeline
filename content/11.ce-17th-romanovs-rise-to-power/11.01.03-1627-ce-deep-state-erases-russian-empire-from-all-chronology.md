## Arabic on Russian Weapons

Among the most striking physical evidence: the Kremlin Armoury Chamber. As late as mid-17th century, Russian weapons bore **Arabic inscriptions** — including the "Jericho Cap" helmet of Czar Mikhail, engraved with Quranic verses. Paul of Aleppo wrote Russian in Arabic letters as late as 1656. Russian Imperial coins were **bilingual** — Khan's name on one side, Grand Prince's on the other, because they were the same person in two languages.

All of this dissolved after the Romanovs. Arabic was stripped, coins redesigned, bilingual records reclassified as "Tatar occupation" artifacts. ([Chronologia, Ch.7 §8–10](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/07_08.html))
