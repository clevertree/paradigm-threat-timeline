# London 1664-1694: Financial Coup

Between 1664 and 1694, London experienced the most concentrated destabilization sequence in its history — a thirty-year arc transforming the last independent financial center in Europe into headquarters of a centralized banking system that would eventually govern the world.

This was not coincidence. It was a coordinated campaign:

1. **1664–65**: A [great comet](/timeline/evt-the-great-comet-of-1664-1665) appeared for over 80 days, interpreted as divine judgment
2. **1664–66**: The [Great Plague](/timeline/evt-the-great-plague-of-1664-1666) killed 15% of London's population, devastating guild and goldsmith communities
3. **Sept 2–6, 1666**: The [Great Fire](/timeline/evt-sept-6-1666-ce-london-burns-to-the-ground) destroyed 13,200 houses, 87 parish churches, the Guildhall, St. Paul's Cathedral, and the Royal Exchange — the physical infrastructure of the old financial order
4. **1666**: The [Cestui Que Vie Act](/timeline/evt-sept-6-1666-ce-london-burns-to-the-ground) — a legal mechanism to declare absent persons dead and their estates managed by others
5. **1667**: The [Dutch burned the English fleet](/timeline/evt-dutch-burn-the-english-fleet) — military humiliation leading to Clarendon's fall
6. **1668–74**: Charles II ruled through [the Cabal](/timeline/evt-the-cabal-englands-shadow-cabinet) — five non-Anglican ministers, two secret Catholics, in a Protestant nation
7. **1672**: The [Stop of the Exchequer](/timeline/evt-ce-deep-state-targets-independent-banks) — Charles suspended all payments, ruining independent goldsmiths and destroying 10,000 families
8. **1694**: The [Bank of England](/timeline/evt-deep-state-solidifies-central-banking) was founded on a Mithras temple — centralizing all currency under a joint-stock corporation answerable to shareholders, not citizens. Charles II implemented William Paterson's plan: the Governor and Company of the Bank of England was chartered as a joint-stock company with limited liability; 1,268 investors raised £1.2 million in 11 days for 8% interest. The Bank was established in an old Roman temple on Walbrook Street where Mithras, god of contracts, was worshipped.

Britannica confirms the sequence was recognized at the time:

> *"The Great Plague of London (1664–66) and the Great Fire of London (1666) were interpreted as divine judgments against a sinful nation. These catastrophes were compounded when the Dutch burned a large portion of the English fleet in 1667, which led to the dismissal and exile of Clarendon. The crown's debts led to the Stop of the Exchequer (1672), by which Charles suspended payment of his bills."* — [Britannica, "The Later Stuarts"](https://www.britannica.com/place/United-Kingdom/The-later-Stuarts)

## The Goldsmiths: The Real Target

The London [goldsmiths](/timeline/evt-goldsmiths-and-the-death-of-independent-valuation) were not merely wealthy merchants. They were the last independent financial power in Europe — inheritors of the old Imperial treasury infrastructure that Fomenko identifies as moved to England by the Reformers ([Ch.7 §18](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/07_15.html)). Their ability to independently appraise gold and determine goods' relative value made them a direct threat to royal currency. As long as independent valuation existed, no centralized monetary authority could achieve total control.

The gold ended up in London through Imperial trade flows and deliberate transfer of the Horde's treasury. The goldsmiths managed it independently as a professional guild. But the Pyramidal Empire needed to seize control to install the new centralized system. The 1664–1694 sequence was the mechanism.

## The Apocalyptic Context

The entire sequence was framed by an [apocalyptic prophecy](/timeline/evt-the-apocalypse-prophecy-of-1666): 1666 was read as "the 666th year of our Lord" — the number of the beast from Revelation. London's leadership was told of impending divine judgment. When each catastrophe arrived — comet, plague, fire — it confirmed the prophecy, silencing political will of those who might have resisted.

## Key Episodes (Condensed)

**Comet (1664–65):** The largest of the series lasted over 80 days; Morton and contemporaries read it as divine forewarning. Per NC, the Almagest star catalogue was finalized in the 17th c.; the comet–plague–fire sequence cleared the ground for the new financial architecture.

**Plague (1664–66):** 15% of London died; St. Giles parish, overcrowded tenements, open sewage. The guild-based City dominated by goldsmiths saw population decimated and institutional memory disrupted. London's geography — wall, Thames, density — made it structurally vulnerable to both epidemic and fire.

**Dutch attack (1667):** Raid on the Medway — Dutch broke the chain, burned/captured warships, towed the *Royal Charles*. Clarendon dismissed and exiled; the Cabal filled the vacuum. Whether rival aristocracy exploiting weakness or coordinated, the consequences served the new order: removal of the old minister, space for the Cabal.

**Stop of the Exchequer (1672):** Charles borrowed from goldsmiths against "tax futures" at discount; by 1671 the discount exceeded the 6% usury cap. Charles suspended all payments — the Great Stop ruined most goldsmiths and 10,000 families. Independent banking crushed; the path to the Bank of England (1694) was clear.
