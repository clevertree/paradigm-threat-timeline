# The Cabal: England's Shadow Cabinet (1668-1674)

After Clarendon's fall in 1667, Charles II governed through five ministers whose collective name became one of the most enduring words in English: **the Cabal**.

| Letter | Minister | Religion | Role |
|--------|----------|----------|------|
| **C** | Sir Thomas [Clifford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Clifford,_1st_Baron_Clifford_of_Chudleigh) | **Secret Catholic** | Lord Treasurer |
| **A** | Henry [Arlington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Bennet,_1st_Earl_of_Arlington) | **Secret Catholic** | Secretary of State |
| **B** | Duke of [Buckingham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Villiers,_2nd_Duke_of_Buckingham) | Libertine | Privy Council |

| **L** | [Lauderdale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Maitland,_1st_Duke_of_Lauderdale) | Presbyterian → Episcopalian | Secretary for Scotland |

**None of the five was Anglican** in a nation whose established church was the Church of England. Two were **secret Catholics** where Catholicism was formally proscribed. This was crypto-governance — a cabinet whose true allegiances were hidden from the governed.

> *"The king now ruled through a group of ministers known as the Cabal, an anagram of the first letters of their names. None of the five was Anglican, and two were Roman Catholic."* — [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/place/United-Kingdom/The-later-Stuarts)

## From Acronym to Accusation

"Cabal" entered English as a term for **any secretive political faction operating behind the scenes**. Its adoption reflected public awareness that England was governed by a shadow cabinet with hidden allegiances. The concept persists — from [Illuminati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illuminati) fears to modern "shadow government" theories — originating in this specific historical moment.

## An Entity Without a Center

The Cabal had five men with five agendas — Clifford wanted Catholic restoration, Shaftesbury wanted parliamentary supremacy, Buckingham wanted personal power. No single "plan" — a convergence of interests producing coordinated action without unified command.

This is how all cabals operate: **no clear center**, **frequent rebranding** — from "the Cabal" to "the establishment" to "the Pyramidal Empire" to "the globalists." They **appear as saviours whenever possible** — presenting financial predation as "modernization," surveillance as "security," monopolies as "efficiency."

## The Question That Won't Go Away

If such an entity had ended, it would have been **defeated and exposed** at some point in three centuries. The 19th–21st century tools of surveillance and communication are powerful enough to expose any purely human organization operating at scale. But that never happened.

Nobody knows who runs the so-called Cabal. The closest we get are institutional names — the Bank for International Settlements, the Federal Reserve, the City of London Corporation, the Vatican Bank — but these are **instruments**, not controllers. An organization maintaining coherent strategy across four centuries, surviving every revolution, adapting to every technology, and never decisively exposed — despite satellites, signals intelligence, and digital finance — either represents an unprecedented achievement in human organizational theory, or something else entirely.

## The Cabal and the Financial Coup

Within the 1664–1694 financial coup, the Cabal **governed England during the transition** between destruction of the old financial order (the [goldsmiths](/content/11.ce-17th-romanovs-rise-to-power/11.04.05-the-goldsmiths-and-the-death-of-independent-valuation.md)) and installation of the new (the Bank of England). Clarendon represented the last link to an older, more accountable governance. His removal ensured no powerful minister could resist the financial restructuring. Not coincidental.
