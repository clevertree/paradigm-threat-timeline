# The Dutch Burn the English Fleet (1667)

In June 1667, during the [Raid on the Medway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raid_on_the_Medway), the Dutch Republic's navy sailed up the Thames estuary into the River Medway, broke through the chain protecting the English fleet, burned or captured several major warships, and towed away the *Royal Charles* — the flagship that had carried Charles II home for his Restoration just seven years earlier. It was the worst military humiliation in English naval history.

The immediate political consequence was the dismissal and exile of the [Earl of Clarendon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Hyde,_1st_Earl_of_Clarendon), Charles II's chief minister, blamed for the disaster. This created the vacuum filled by [the Cabal](/timeline/evt-the-cabal-englands-shadow-cabinet) — the five non-Anglican ministers who would govern England through Charles for the next six years.

> *"The Great Plague of London (1664–66) and the Great Fire of London (1666) were interpreted as divine judgments against a sinful nation. These catastrophes were compounded when the Dutch burned a large portion of the English fleet in 1667, which led to the dismissal and exile of Clarendon."* — [Britannica, "The Later Stuarts"](https://www.britannica.com/place/United-Kingdom/The-later-Stuarts)

## A Rival Puppet, Not a Pyramidal Empire Operation

I interpret this as a **rival puppet European aristocracy taking advantage of a moment of weakness** in Britain's rulership, having little to do with any Pyramidal Empire theory. The Dutch Republic — itself the product of the same Reformation that fractured the Hordian Empire — was a competing state with its own interests, aristocracy, and ambitions.

People were not aware of any Pyramidal Empire or cabal operating behind the scenes. We were like **insects under a microscope** — we can see the glass, but everything beyond it is a blur. We see many moments of weakness in history — especially modern history, where civil war or self-sabotage from the highest levels of leadership leaves nations temporarily vulnerable — and other countries stepping in to take advantage.

The pattern repeats: China invaded Vietnam on [February 17, 1979](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Vietnamese_War), exploiting Vietnam War aftermath and Vietnam's intervention in Cambodia. Argentina seized the Falklands in 1982 during perceived British weakness. Saddam Hussein invaded Kuwait in 1990 calculating international attention was elsewhere. In 1667, the Dutch saw an England ravaged by plague, devastated by fire, and politically divided — and struck.

This does not make the Dutch attack part of a larger conspiracy. It makes it part of human nature: when a rival is weakened, opportunists advance. The *consequences* of the attack, however, served the Pyramidal Empire's interests perfectly — removing Clarendon and opening space for the Cabal. Whether this was foreseen or merely exploited after the fact is an open question.
