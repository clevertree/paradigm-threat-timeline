# 17th Century: From Empire to Invisible Government

The 17th century is the hinge of modern history — when **visible empire** was replaced by **invisible government**. At its start, a single world-ruling dynasty controlled the administrative, military, and financial infrastructure of history's largest empire. By its end, that empire had fractured into competing nation-states, its treasury moved to London under centralized banking control, and the orchestrating entity had made itself invisible.

## The Thesis

The 17th century began with a world-empire and ended with a world-bank. The empire was visible — armies marched, emperors sat on thrones, borders were mapped. The bank was invisible — shareholders anonymous, authority derived from royal charter, control extending over every nation-state adopting centralized monetary authority.

Every major power structure governing today — central banking, the nation-state system, Westphalian sovereignty, Anglican/Protestant religious framework, Jesuit educational network — was created or consolidated in this century.
