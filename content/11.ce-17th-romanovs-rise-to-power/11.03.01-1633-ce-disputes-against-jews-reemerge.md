# Disputes against Jews reemerge

The breaking up of the Russian Empire created the [Jewish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jewish_diaspora) and [Gypsy / Romani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romani_diaspora) diasporas. Both groups had been accustomed to traveling between vassal states of the Empire and now found themselves cut off from old imperial ties. Jews found themselves at the highest level of authority in each region — controlling money, trade economies, and [monarchies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchies_in_Europe) left behind — while Gypsies, named after an Empire that no longer existed, became powerless. Eventually, disputes between common folk (gentiles) and Jews emerged over every facet of life, from [eating rituals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kashrut) to [monetary policy](https://mosaicmagazine.com/observation/history-ideas/2019/06/filthy-lucre-a-look-at-the-history-of-jews-and-money/).

## The Imperial Treasury Caste

The New Chronology offers a structural explanation for why the Jewish community survived the Empire's collapse when so many other groups did not. In the Hordian Empire, social organization followed a hereditary caste model with different clans occupying different professional roles across generations. The Hordian financial officers — those managing the Empire's accounts across Europe, Africa, and America, collecting taxes, paying salaries, controlling precious metal flows, financing the army — formed a distinct professional caste. Their work demanded precision, accounting skill, and a strict regulatory mindset (hence the Talmudic legal system's emphasis on financial rules).

This caste operated globally by necessity: an Imperial banker in Egypt and one in England had stronger professional ties than two soldiers in distant provinces. Over generations, this tight-knit professional guild developed its own religious community, which became a recognizable strand of what we now call Judaism. Crucially, this guild needed no territorial state — their "country" was the Empire itself, granting free movement across all provinces. When the Empire collapsed, they retained cosmopolitan mobility and professional networks, while warrior castes and administrative nobles were territorialized and eventually replaced by new local dynasties. ([Chronologia, Ch.9 §3](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/09.html))

## Power of Money Erupts

Fomenko identifies the critical transition: in the 16th and early 17th centuries, as the Empire fractured, the previously subordinate financial caste found itself in unprecedented power. The military governors of new independent states — the kings of Germany, France, England — believed they were the victors. They were not. The driving force behind the revolutionary wave sweeping Europe (English Revolution, French Revolution, Dutch Republic before them) was capital overriding nobility. The [Miser Knight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Covetous_Knight) pattern became the new political reality: the Imperial knight demanding his rightful salary from the treasury official who now controlled the cash.

> *"The driving force behind all such revolutions was money. As a result they openly declared the domination of money over noble rank, over ancestry. This was the motto of the French revolution and of the English revolution. In the Horde Empire there ruled a principle of ancestry and gentility. Nobility was respected and entitled to power. After the revolt of the Reformation, wealth acquired precedence."* — Fomenko, [Ch.9 §3](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/09.html)

The ghettos appearing across Europe after 1615 were not continuation of ancient prejudice but a new phenomenon — response to the collapse of the Empire whose treasury system the Jewish community had administered. Once Imperial authority was gone, local kingdoms had no institutional framework for integrating former Imperial officials. A 1615 Lutheran Chronograph document records the expulsion of Jews from Worms directly after the Time of Troubles, when the old Imperial order finally dissolved. ([Chronologia, Ch.9 §3](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/09.html))
