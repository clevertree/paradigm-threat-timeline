# European Castles — Fortresses Against Giants

![European Castles — Fortresses Against Giants](/media/08.ce-14th-great-expansion-of-the-mongol-slav/castles-vs-giants.png)

## The Standard Narrative Falls Apart

Mainstream history frames European castles as feudal military strongholds for human warfare. One problem: **most castles in England, France, Ireland, and Scotland were never involved in conflicts or sieges.** Wikipedia, citing Coulson (2003), admits the "domestic life" of castles is "a neglected facet."

When gunpowder artillery arrived (15th century), castles became indefensible overnight. Constantinople's walls fell to Ottoman cannon in **six weeks** (1453). Why were these immense structures so poorly optimised against artillery? Because they were designed against **giants**.

## The Castle-Building Window and the Giant Timeline

Peak castle construction spans the **11th through 14th centuries** — precisely when the [Rus-Horde Empire and its giant leadership caste](/content/07.ce-13th-the-russian-horde-tartarian-empire/07.01.02-the-giants-of-the-rus-horde.md) were active. The [Battle of Kulikovo (1380)](/content/08.ce-14th-great-expansion-of-the-mongol-slav/08.02.00-1380-ce-giants-are-defeated-at-the-battle-of-kulikovo.md) marked the decisive defeat of giants by cannon — and castle construction entered sharp decline immediately after.

## Features That Make Sense Against Giants

**Elevation and Inaccessibility:** Hilltops, cliff edges, rocky outcrops, tidal islands — maximising difficulty for large, heavy creatures.

**Wall Thickness Over Height:** Walls typically **3 metres thick**, 12 metres tall. Against humans with ladders, thickness is wasted. Against a giant who can **strike or push** a wall, mass is critical.

**Narrow Entrances and Passages:** Lengthened passages, multiple portcullises, sharp turns — a creature of enormous size **physically cannot fit** through turning passages.

**Moats:** Human armies routinely bridged or drained them. Against a giant, a deep ditch is devastating: a creature weighing several times more than a human sinks in soft ground and becomes exposed.

**Keeps and Tower Houses:** Keep entrances on the **second storey** (accessible only by removable ladders), spiral staircases that **physically exclude** any oversized creature.

## Castle Stalker — Case Study

[Castle Stalker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castle_Stalker) is a tower house on a **tidal islet** in Loch Laich, Scotland — built c. 1320. Maximally defensible against large land creatures, minimally defensible against humans with boats. In 700 years it has **never been sieged** — it changed hands c. 1620 through a **drunken bet**. The name derives from Gaelic *Stalcaire* ("hunter"), suggesting a hunting lodge where the "wildlife" required stone walls and a tidal moat.

## The Pattern Across Europe

Hundreds of tower houses across Scotland, Ireland, Wales, and western France share this profile: remote inaccessible locations, disproportionately thick walls, virtually no military history. Wikipedia estimates **75,000–100,000 castles** across western Europe — defying purely military explanation for inter-human warfare, but sensible for a continent shared with giants.

## The Gunpowder Transition

Once cannon proliferated, castles were abandoned wholesale — replaced by low-profile star forts with completely different philosophy. This discontinuity is not evolution but **replacement**: two engineering responses to two different threats. Once [cannon defeated giants at Kulikovo (1380)](/content/08.ce-14th-great-expansion-of-the-mongol-slav/08.02.00-1380-ce-giants-are-defeated-at-the-battle-of-kulikovo.md), castles had served their purpose.
