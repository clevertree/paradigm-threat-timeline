## The Davidovich Discovery

French chemist Joseph Davidovich founded the Geopolymer Institute in 1979 and proved through years of laboratory analysis that the blocks of the Great Pyramid — along with countless Egyptian statues, sarcophagi, and vessels — are **artificial stone** ([Davidovich, refs [1086]–[1093] in Fomenko](https://chronologia.org/en/chronologia5/empire19_07.html)). The method: grind soft limestone into powder, mix with Nile clay (aluminium oxide), natron (sodium carbonate), and water. The slurry is poured into wooden moulds and hardens at room temperature into stone indistinguishable from natural rock.

This is not modern grey concrete. Geopolymeric concrete can replicate **any geological material** — limestone, granite, diorite, basalt. As Davidovich wrote: *"Geologists unfamiliar with the capabilities of geopolymerisation mistake geopolymeric concrete for natural stone."*

## Herodotus Describes Moulds, Not Block-Dragging

Fomenko points out that Herodotus's famous description of pyramid construction — when read carefully — describes **mobile wooden casings** used to lift and cast concrete blocks level by level ([chronologia.org Ch. 19 §6](https://chronologia.org/en/chronologia5/empire19_06.html)). Egyptologists interpreted "wooden machines" as ramps and sledges. But the text describes casings moved from tier to tier — casting moulds.

## The Physical Evidence

- **Matting marks:** Every block examined at heights above 50 metres shows textile impressions from casting moulds — the weave pattern of the mould lining
- **Uniform block composition:** Chemical analysis shows blocks contain materials not found in nearby quarries, but consistent with a manufactured mixture
- **Mould seams:** The unfinished quartzite head of Nefertiti preserves a casting seam running down the facial centreline — forehead, nose, chin — visible proof it was cast in a two-part mould ([chronologia.org Ch. 19 §7](https://chronologia.org/en/chronologia5/empire19_07.html))
- **Diorite vessels:** Narrow-necked vessels of rock harder than iron, with uniform wall thickness and no tool marks — impossible to carve, trivial to cast on a potter's wheel
- **No chisel jitter:** Deep hieroglyphic inscriptions in hard granite show no tool deviation, even when crossing hard mineral inclusions — consistent with inscription in soft concrete before hardening

## A Lost Technology

Geopolymer concrete was one of many technologies lost when the Horde Empire fragmented in the 17th century. Like Damascus steel, Rostov enamel, and tula granulation, the formula was a **state secret** known to a handful of craftsmen. When the Empire fell, the knowledge holders were killed or refused to share with conquerors. Alchemical texts encoding the recipe survived, but later generations — having lost the context — read them as mysticism. Davidovich's work rediscovered a technology that was never magic, only forgotten.

## This Timeline's Position

Fomenko dates pyramid construction to the XIV–XVI centuries CE as part of the Horde's monumental building programme. This timeline accepts Davidovich's geopolymer evidence but **disagrees with Fomenko's dating**. The knowledge of geopolymer concrete was not a medieval alchemical invention — it was inherited from the Golden Age, carried into the Dark Ages by survivors of the cataclysm. Those who built the pyramids did so **as early as necessary**, applying techniques already perfected in an earlier epoch that Fomenko's framework does not account for.
