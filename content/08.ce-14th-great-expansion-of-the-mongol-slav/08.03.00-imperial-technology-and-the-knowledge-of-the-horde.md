# Imperial Technology and the Knowledge of the Horde

![Imperial Technology and the Knowledge of the Horde](/media/08.ce-14th-great-expansion-of-the-mongol-slav/imperial-technology.png)

The Great 14th-century Expansion was technological conquest as much as military. The Horde's ability to build monuments still standing, forge metals modern metallurgists struggle to replicate, and project force across three continents rested on unified knowledge Scaligerian history fragmented into "Greek," "Arabic," "Egyptian," "Indian," and "Chinese" strands. Fomenko restores the picture: one Empire, one knowledge tradition, deliberately shattered by later chroniclers.

## Geopolymer Concrete and the Philosopher's Stone

Monumental construction of this era — Egyptian pyramids, Hagia Sophia, Baalbek, Great Wall — shares a secret: **geopolymer concrete**. Fomenko identifies this as the **Philosopher's Stone** ([chronologia.org Ch. 4 §18](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/04_14.html)). Not mystical — a chemical binder that, added to crushed stone debris and water, produced artificial stone indistinguishable from natural rock. The Horde could cast enormous blocks in situ (eliminating impossible quarrying of 1,000+ ton Baalbek monoliths), reproduce any shape, work at enormous scale and speed.

The "alchemical" tradition — obsessive coded search for transformative substance — is garbled memory of this lost technology. When Empire fractured in 17th century and formula was lost, later scholars could only interpret surviving texts as mysticism.

## Cannon Technology — The Cross and the Cannon

Fomenko connects cannon directly to the **Cross** ([chronologia.org Ch. 4 §27](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/04_14.html)): Russian word for cannon (*pushka*) and cross-shaped gun carriage are linked. Imperial banners carried the cross as **symbol of their decisive weapon**. Combined with [Damask steel](/timeline?event=evt-hundred-years-war-ends), cannon artillery made Horde unstoppable by early 15th century.

## The "Arabic Golden Age" — A Single Knowledge Base

No separate "Arab" civilization — Arabic-speaking scholars were part of same Empire. Arabic inscriptions appear on Russian imperial weapons of 14th–16th centuries. Horde coins bear both Slavonic and Arabic. "Damascus steel" derives from Mosokh = Moscow, not Syrian city. "Greek → Arabic → Latin" translation chain was not civilizations but *administrative languages* within one Empire.

The "Islamic Golden Age" dissolves into what it was: **scientific wing of the Horde**, staffed by scholars writing in Arabic (one of Empire's co-languages alongside Slavonic, Turkic, Greek). When Empire split, each successor state claimed "its own" ancient tradition — Arabs took Arabic texts, Greeks the Greek ones, Latins the translations.

## Roman Numerals — Carpenter Marks

Fomenko demonstrates Roman numerals originated as **Russian carpenter and builder marks** ([chronologia.org Ch. 4 §20](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/04_14.html)) — practical tally notation for marking timber and stone. These marks spread across Europe during Great Expansion and were later attributed to fictional "Ancient Rome."

## Alchemy and Metallurgy

Medieval alchemy was **degraded residue of Horde industrial metallurgy**: tin, copper, bronze on industrial scale; iron smelting for cannon and armour; "transmutation of metals" encoding real processes like cupellation (extracting gold from lead ore). When Empire fragmented, metallurgical techniques became orphaned trade secrets, surviving only as cryptic manuscripts later generations read as mysticism.

## The Astronomical Foundation

Fomenko's statistical analysis of Ptolemy's *Almagest* demonstrates its star catalogue dates to **medieval period** (~600–1300 CE), not 2nd century AD ([chronologia.org Ch. 4 §37](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/04_34.html)). Astronomical knowledge was collected by Horde-era observers — essential for navigation and fleet movements. Backdating by ~1,200 years was part of programme creating fictional "Ancient World."
