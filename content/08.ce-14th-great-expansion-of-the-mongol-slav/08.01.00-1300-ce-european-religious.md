# 1300 CE — European Religious Schism

The Great Empire initially practiced a state-tolerated folk spirituality integrating planetary veneration, ancestor worship, and pragmatic governance. As the Empire expanded, a systematic split emerged: **Royal Christianity** and **Apostolic Christianity** — two competing ideological systems that eventually produced rival civilizations.

---

## Royal Christianity

The rulers' ideology, directly serving Imperial legitimacy. Core tenets:

1. **The Emperor is the divinely appointed Christ/Czar** — God's authority on Earth through Imperial lineage
2. **Religious and civil law are inseparable** — The Empire embodies both spiritual and legal order
3. **Centralized hierarchy** — local governors are extensions of Imperial will

Royal Christianity prioritized **order, tribute, and expansion**. It was the internal ideology of the ruling class, not a missionary project.

---

## Apostolic Christianity

A parallel movement emerged privileging **teaching over governance** and **apostolic succession over imperial appointment**. Core tenets:

1. **Authority derives from Peter and the Apostles** — not from emperors
2. **The spiritual hierarchy is independent of civil power** — bishops answer to Rome/Constantinople, not local governors
3. **Universal mission** — Apostles' Christianity is for all peoples, not just Imperial subjects

---

## The Structural Schism

The division's significance: **it created two branches of government**.

Royal Christians saw the schism as rebellion — provincial governors claiming autonomy under religious pretext.

Apostolic Christians saw it as fidelity to Christ over Caesar — the Empire's corruption required an independent church.

This tension intensified from 1300 CE onward as Europe fractured under internal conflict and the Apostolic institutions accumulated their own power centers.

## The Deep State Exploits the Schism

On the Deep State's formation and CE role, see [Humanity cast out](/timeline/evt-humanity-was-then-cast-out-of-the-garden-of-eden) and [Deep State, Census, Naming](/timeline/evt-the-deep-state-the-census-and-the-naming-of-christ). Fomenko identifies "Judaic capital" (what we call the Deep State) as exploiting this division. Pre-existing financial networks operating across Imperial borders found opportunity in religious fragmentation. Strategy:

1. **Finance both sides** — Protestant, Catholic, and Orthodox factions all needed capital
2. **Weaponize religious difference** — Wars justify borrowing and create debt cycles
3. **Survive regime changes** — Networks persist while dynasties rise and fall

The Deep State's power emerged not from conquering territory but from **becoming indispensable to everyone who did**.

---

## Seeds of 1492

By the 14th century the structural preconditions for the Empire's fragmentation were established:

- **Royal vs. Apostolic** conflict provided permanent fault line
- **Financial networks** positioned themselves as power brokers
- **Regional governors** recognized the potential of independence under Apostolic legitimacy

The Great Expansion (1300s–1400s) paradoxically accelerated the fracture: the farther the Empire spread, the harder Rus-Horde found it to enforce loyalty. The provinces that would become Western Europe began forming distinct identities — ideologically, financially, and eventually militarily positioned for the 16th-century breakaway.
