# Authors Challenging the Mainstream Timeline

The following researchers have made primary contributions to demonstrating that Scaligerian chronology — the conventional historical timeline — is fabricated, inflated, or systematically wrong.

Their methods vary: mathematical/statistical analysis, astronomical reanalysis of eclipse records, re-reading of archaeological stratigraphy, and forensic examination of institutional suppression. What unites them is the conclusion that accepted ancient history dates are largely invented.

**Note:** Researchers who challenge the timeline AND interpret ancient sources as cataclysm (Fomenko, Heinsohn, Schoch, Hancock, Cremo) are listed in [Authors Who Read Ancient Sources as World Cataclysm](/content/00.overview/00.02.02-authors-cataclysm-texts.md).

## N. A. Morozov (1854–1946)

Russian mathematician, astronomer, political prisoner. Confined 27 years in the Peter and Paul Fortress and Schlisselburg Prison, he produced a 7-volume work analyzing astronomical content of ancient documents — eclipses, stellar positions, comet appearances — finding that dates assigned by Scaligerian chronology are mathematically impossible. The astronomical data, re-analyzed without presupposing conventional dates, consistently yields medieval dates, not classical antiquity.

Morozov's conclusions:
- **Most of classical antiquity is illusion** — "ancient" eclipse records date to the early medieval period
- **The Old Testament is a medieval document** — astronomical content inconsistent with 1st millennium BCE
- **Ancient Greece and Rome** as standardly dated have no records consistent with their asserted periods

His work directly inspired Fomenko.

**Key works:** *Christ* (7 volumes, 1924–1932)

## Heribert Illig (b. 1947)

German independent historian whose 1991 *Das erfundene Mittelalter* proposed that years 614–911 CE were deliberately inserted — creating 297 phantom years — by Otto III, Pope Sylvester II, and Constantine VII to place their reign at the symbolic millennium.

The evidential core:
- **No unambiguous archaeological/astronomical evidence** independently dates to 614–911 CE
- **Carolingian architecture** shows stylistic anachronisms
- **Calendar drift** — Julian calendar's accumulated error by 1582 should be larger; the shortfall matches 297 phantom years

Illig focuses on deliberate political forgery rather than Fomenko's error-accumulation model. Both frameworks are broadly compatible.

**Key works:** *Das erfundene Mittelalter* (1991), *Wer hat an der Uhr gedreht?* (1999)
