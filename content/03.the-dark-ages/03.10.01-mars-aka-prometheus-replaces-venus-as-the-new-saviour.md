# Mars (aka Prometheus) replaces Venus as the new Saviour

> *Each new saviour planet carried a dual nature: first as liberator, then — as it too caused catastrophe — becoming the new archetype of evil. Mars is mythology's most ironic case: the dragon-slayer who was also the dragon.*
>

## Mars the Saviour: Prometheus, the Fire-Giver

When Venus settled into a less catastrophic orbit after the Exodus, **Priori-Mars** appeared in geosynchronous orbit above the northern hemisphere, looming enormous in the sky. After comet-Venus's terror, a relatively stable, brilliant red body was relief.

In the [Greek narrative](https://www.ancient.eu/Prometheus/), Prometheus (Mars) forced humans to invent technology too early, thwarting Jupiter's plan. Electric encounters with Mars quickly taught humans to harness electricity. In the 7th century BCE, humanity enjoyed free energy, ideal health, and no Pyramidal influence as [magnificent cities](https://www.cnn.com/travel/article/worlds-best-ancient-cities/index.html) blossomed worldwide.

Mars-as-saviour across cultures:
- **Greek**: Prometheus — stole fire from the gods for humanity
- **Roman**: Mars Ultor ("Mars the Avenger") — champion against cosmic tyranny
- **Norse**: Tyr — one-handed god of war and justice
- **Hindu**: Karttikeya / Skanda — divine warrior overthrowing demonic forces
- **Hebrew**: The Angel Michael — warrior archangel who defeated the Dragon (Venus)

Mars's gift was **fire and electricity** — practical knowledge of plasma, demonstrating that the Pyramidal Empire was mortal and defeatable.

## Mars the Destroyer: The Red Dragon

Yet Mars was simultaneously the **most feared object in the ancient sky**. In geosynchronous orbit just 42,000 kilometres above Earth, it periodically exchanged electric discharges causing earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, tsunamis, and global electromagnetic storms.

The ancient world's most vivid monsters are Mars:
- **The Red Dragon** (Revelation 12) — Mars's plasma tail
- **Typhon** (Greek), **Vritra** (Hindu), **Apep** (Egyptian) — the great serpent-monster
- **Set** (Egyptian) — red-skinned god of chaos, enemy of Osiris and Horus
- **Ares** (Greek) — despised even by his father Zeus

Homer writes that Zeus says of Ares: *"of all the gods who hold Olympus, I hate you the most."* He was the saviour who terrified his worshippers.

## The Nine Deaths of Mars

With each encounter, [Priori-Mars](https://cycliccatastrophism.org/2010/01/16/the-origin-of-avatar/)'s outer shell collapsed, making the inner shell temporarily visible. The legend of Prometheus eternally chained — his liver torn out each day by an eagle, regenerated each night, torn out again — is mythological memory of repeated encounters: a planet seeming to die and be reborn nine times.

On **February 27, 747 BCE**, Mars bumped Earth's magnetosphere and altered Earth's orbital duration. Calendar keepers introduced a [leap year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leap_year) every 4 years for the new 6-hour offset. Even this final gift — calendar stabilization — was delivered through catastrophe.

With Mars's final departure, the sky emptied of terrifying close-encounter planets. A new, reliable light source stepped forward to fill the theological vacuum.
