# Hairy Mary: The Wild Woman Who Threatened Patriarchal Religion

![Hairy Mary](/media/06.ce-12th-birth-of-christianity/hairy-mary.png)
*Wild Women were portrayed with full body hair, nursing children, living outside Church society. The iconography encodes memory of a real human phenotype.*

## Why Was Christ's Mother Depicted as Hairy?

Art historian Magdalena Łanuszka [rejects](https://en.posztukiwania.pl/2018/11/10/hairy-mary-one-more-time/) the standard explanation that medieval artists confused Mary Magdalene with Mary of Egypt:

> "I am pretty convinced that the medieval viewer, looking at a hirsute Mary Magdalene, most likely saw her as a **Wild Woman**!"

Wild Men and Wild Women (Wildweibchen) were a distinct iconographic tradition — hairy, self-sufficient humans in wilderness. The [Met Museum](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/364585) holds Master ES's "Wild Woman with Unicorn." In medieval art, Wild Men "symbolised **sinful humanity**" — exactly what we'd expect if the Church sought to demonize a population it intended to eliminate. Łanuszka notes the hirsute Magdalene vanished from Western art when Wild Men lost popularity — the tradition ended because living memory of actual hairy humans was extinguished.

## The Pregnant Hairy Woman Fleeing the Census

No census has required pregnant women to travel. Mary was not traveling *to* the census — she was **fleeing** it. The census targeted her kind: hairy giants from Rus'. In 15th-century Netherlandish paintings, Magdalene appears in **unlaced maternity dresses** — the period's pregnancy signal. In Rogier van der Weyden's *Descent from the Cross*, [Magdalene wears a belt inscribed **"JHESUS MARIA"**](https://en.posztukiwania.pl/2014/07/22/pregnant-mary-magdalene/) — as if the unborn child bore its parents' names.

Christ's father is absent from every Gospel. The Church's "divine conception" may overlay simpler reality: the father was killed or captured in the census-genocide. **Marriage as institution was created by the same Church hunting her people.** Among self-sufficient, matrilineal Wild People, Church-sanctioned union meant nothing.

## The Existential Threat: Matrilineal vs Patrilineal Religion

Christ's mother threatened the nascent Church because of **lineage**. In eastern Rus' tradition, religious heritage passed through the **mother**. The Church was building a **patriarchal religion** where authority flowed from God the Father to Christ the Son to male priests. A prominent female figure passing religious authority through the maternal line collapsed their claim.

The Church's solution: conflate all Marys into one domesticated Virgin; split threatening aspects into a separate sinful penitent; eliminate the Wild People — removing living evidence of the old matrilineal tradition.

## The Trojan War: A War Over the "Woman"

Per [Fomenko's reconstruction](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/03.html), the Bible and chronicles used "wives" to represent **religions**: "the cause of the Trojan = Tarquinian = Gothic war could have been a **RELIGIOUS DISPUTE**: whose 'wife' was better." The "woman" at the centre of the Trojan War was Mary — the religious tradition of Rus'. The conflict was between Eastern/matrilineal tradition and Western/patrilineal Deep State priesthood.

The hairy giants were dangerous because they were **self-sufficient** — able to live without buildings, money, or Church infrastructure. Christ's mother, fleeing the census with her unborn child, represented an older humanity that threatened the patriarchal church by existing.
