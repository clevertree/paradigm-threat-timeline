The words [describe a cesarean section](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/02.html). Greek PARTHENOS (virgin) originates from Slavonic POROT', meaning "to cut open." The claim Mary "remained a virgin after giving birth" is literally true under cesarean: the birth canal was never used.

According to [eastern chronicles](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/02_18.html), the procedure stabbed infant Christ while still in the womb, leading to traditions of the [Holy Lance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Lance) and [Holy Grail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Grail). This birth wound may have been conflated with the lance of the Roman soldier at crucifixion.

In Christ's epoch, Rus' adopted Christianity wholly and instantly. Fomenko [identifies the Magi](https://chronologia.org/en/how_it_was/02.html) as three Rus-Horde rulers: Russian Czar Vladimir (Balthazar), Czaritsa Malka — his mother (Melchior), and Cossack Ataman (Caspar). A Cologne Cathedral was erected in their honor, housing the Shrine of the Three Kings. Medieval images depict adoration against banners displaying *a crescent and a star* — symbol of Czar-Grad (Jerusalem). In the Cologne Shrine, two Magi are bearded men, but the third is *clearly female* — Queen Malka, later masculinized by Western artists uncomfortable with Slavic origins.
