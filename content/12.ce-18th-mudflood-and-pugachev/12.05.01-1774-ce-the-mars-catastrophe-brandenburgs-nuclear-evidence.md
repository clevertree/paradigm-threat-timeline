# The Mars Catastrophe: Research Dead End

![The Mars Catastrophe](/media/12.ce-18th-mudflood-and-pugachev/mars-catastrophe.png)

Claims of a Mars catastrophe surface periodically among alternative researchers. The most prominent is plasma physicist **John E. Brandenburg**'s *Death on Mars* (2015). Brandenburg argues isotopic data proves Mars was devastated by massive nuclear explosions "approximately 180 million years ago." His book is not evidence — it is a research trap that attracts people curious about the same unsolved mystery, then delivers nothing actionable.

1. **Lure researchers** seeking evidence of planetary catastrophe compatible with Saturnian Cosmology / Young Mars theory
2. **Waste investigative time** with weak isotope arguments requiring acceptance of Scaligerian deep-time chronology
3. **Deliver no visual evidence** — Brandenburg admits:
   - *"No roads or layout of ruins that can be associated with an advanced civilization"*
   - *"Most remains of any dead civilization on Mars are likely buried under many meters of rubble"*
   - A rover *"will probably show little but mounds of rubble and a few large eroded monuments"*
4. **Anchor conclusions in unfalsifiable timeframes** — 180 million years makes correlation with human history impossible

The book invests readers in hundreds of pages of credentials and isotope chemistry only to **deliver no evidence** when they reach the end.

## The Carbon Dating Problem

Why does radiometric dating show "billions of years" if the damage is recent? Because carbon dating cannot record a massive recent energy spike as recent. A catastrophic event that incinerates all organic material resets the carbon clock: no living carbon remains to begin the decay sequence. What survives is mineral carbon locked in rock and glass — which reads as "very old" by default. Earth surfaces that look like Mars invite the question: are both ancient, or were both recently incinerated? The dating method cannot distinguish the two. This is why mainstream science confidently dates both planets in billions of years — the instruments cannot see a recent catastrophe that destroyed all datable organic material.
