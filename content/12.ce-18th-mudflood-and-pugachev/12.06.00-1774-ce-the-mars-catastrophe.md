## The Carbon Dating Problem

If Mars (and portions of Earth) were recently incinerated — within the last thousand years — why does radiometric dating show "billions of years"? Because carbon dating cannot record a massive recent energy spike as recent. A catastrophic event incinerating all organic material resets the carbon clock to zero: no living carbon remains to begin the decay sequence. What survives is mineral carbon — locked in rock and glass — which reads as "very old" by default. Mainstream science concludes both planets are billions of years old, when the surfaces we photograph may have been melted within recorded human memory. The question is not whether the damage is ancient or recent — it is whether the dating method can distinguish the two.
