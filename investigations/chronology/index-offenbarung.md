# Die Offenbarung Johannis — English Index

**Full title:** *Die Offenbarung Johannis: Eine astronomisch-historische Untersuchung*
**Author:** Nikolaus Morosow
**Published:** Stuttgart, 1912 (W. Spemann). German translation of the Russian original (1907).
**Foreword:** Prof. Dr. Arthur Drews, Karlsruhe

## Summary

Morosow applies astronomical computation to date the Book of Revelation (Apocalypse of John),
concluding it was written on **30 September 395 AD** during a storm on the island of Patmos,
and arguing the author was **John Chrysostom** (Johannes von Antiochien), the famous opponent
of Byzantine political-religious absolutism. The astronomical calculations were independently
verified by astronomers Kamensky and Ljapin of the Pulkowa Observatory (St. Petersburg).

## About the Author


**Nikolaus Morosow** (Nikolai Aleksandrovich Morozov, 1854–1946) was a Russian scientist, revolutionary,
and political prisoner. He spent 25 years in the Schlüsselburg fortress (1882–1905), during which
he wrote this and other works. He was a professor of astronomy, member of nearly all Russian natural
science societies, and a direct intellectual predecessor of Anatoly Fomenko's New Chronology.
The Russian Orthodox Church had this book banned as *'harmful to the Orthodox Church'* (c. 1908).
