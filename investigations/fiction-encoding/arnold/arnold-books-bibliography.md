# Edwin Lester Arnold — Bibliography

**Edwin Lester Linden Arnold** (14 May 1857 – 1 March 1935)  
English writer. Best known for *Lieut. Gullivar Jones: His Vacation* (1905), an early planetary romance and possible influence on Edgar Rice Burroughs's Barsoom series.

---

## Fiction

| Title | Year | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| *The Wonderful Adventures of Phra the Phoenician* | 1890 (US) / 1891 (UK) | First serialised in *Illustrated London News*; historical romance with reincarnation/suspended animation; Phra witnesses invasions of England across centuries. Intro by Sir Edwin Arnold. |
| *The Constable of St. Nicholas* | 1894 | Novel. |
| *The Story of Ulla and Other Tales* | 1895 | Collection. Includes "The Story of Ulla" (*Short Stories*, Mar 1892), "Rutherford the Twice-Born" (*The Idler*, May 1892), and eight other stories. |
| *Lepidus the Centurion: A Roman of Today* | 1901 | Lost-race/adult fantasy; commercially unsuccessful. [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/lepiduscenturio00arnogoog) |
| *Lieut. Gullivar Jones: His Vacation* | 1905 | Planetary romance; magic carpet to Mars; precursor to Burroughs. Republished as *Gulliver of Mars* (Ace 1964), *Gullivar of Mars* (Bison 2003). |

## Life & Context

- **Father:** Sir Edwin Arnold (1832–1904), author of *The Light of Asia* (1879)
- **Childhood:** Largely in India; returned to England for agriculture and ornithology
- **Career:** Journalist from 1883; coffee cultivation in India/Ceylon
- **Themes:** Reincarnation, suspended animation, Buddhism (*Phra*; father's influence)
- **Reception:** *Gullivar Jones* poorly received; Arnold wrote little fiction after 1905; *Phra* and *Gullivar Jones* are his best-known works
