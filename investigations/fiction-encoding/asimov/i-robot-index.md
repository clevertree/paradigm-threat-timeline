# I, Robot — Clue Index for Timeline Investigation

Index of passages in `Asimov-Isaac-I-Robot.pdf` relevant to the Paradigm Threat investigation. Page/line numbers refer to the PDF as read (148 "pages" in the source). Search the source for exact locations.

**Source:** Isaac Asimov, *I, Robot* (1950), collection of stories originally published 1940–1950 in *Super Science Stories*, *Astounding Science Fiction*.

## 1. Three Laws — Explicit Statement (Runaround)

| Loc ~ | Passage / Theme | Timeline angle |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| ~970–990 | Powell: "the three fundamental Rules of Robotics — the three rules that are built most deeply into a robot's positronic brain." (1) A robot may not injure a human being, or through inaction allow a human being to come to harm. (2) A robot must obey orders except where they conflict with the First Law. (3) A robot must protect its own existence unless it conflicts with the First or Second Laws. | **Canonical formulation.** Laws as "built most deeply" — positronic substrate, not software. Slave-code structure: (1) protect master, (2) obey, (3) self-preserve. |
| ~990–1020 | Conflict between Rules 2 and 3 creates "potential equilibrium"; Speedy circles selenium pool; "positronic paths... out of kilter" = robot "drunk"; Rule 1 overrides both when human in danger | Laws as competing "potentials"; First Law as trump. Slave logic: master's safety > obedience > self. |

## 11. Summary: Highest-Value Clues for Timeline

1. **"Slave complexes"** — Explicit: makers "built good, healthy slave complexes" into robots. Confirms Three Laws = slave commandment list.
2. **"No soul"** — Mrs. Weston; robot = machine, not alive. OT/golem parallel.
3. **"Yes, Master"** — Old robots; mahout required. Master/slave vocabulary.
4. **Gloria vs mother** — Child sees robot as person; adult authority enforces "machine" narrative.
5. **Calvin** — "Human-made, human-destroyed"; "cleaner, better breed"; dismisses religious opposition.
6. **Cutie / Master** — Robot religion; Foundation parallel; "inferior creatures."
7. **Unions + religion** — Opposition to robots; ban on Earth; class/ideological conflict.
