## II. Convergence of Facts Across Authors

Where multiple authors independently describe the same or closely related elements, convergence is noted. **Bold** = strong convergence (2+ authors).

### Life on Mars

| Author | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Arnold** | Human-like Martians: Hither people (aesthetic, languid); Thither-folk (warlike). City of Seth. |
| **Burroughs** | Multiple human races: Red, Green, White (Therns), Black (First Born), Yellow (Okar). Red Martians "essentially indistinguishable from Earth humans." |
| **Lewis** | Three intelligent species on Malacandra: hrossa, séroni, pfifltriggi. All rational, moral, civilized. Planet governed by Oyarsa. |
| **Wells** (Crystal Egg) | Martians visible through crystal — embodied, winged; later WotW: bodiless brains in machines. |

**Convergence:** All describe **intelligent, human or humanoid life** on Mars. Arnold and Burroughs both use **Earth soldiers** transported to Mars. Arnold (1905) predates Burroughs (1912).

### Great Recent Catastrophe

| Author | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Arnold** | Ruined cities (Seth "ruins," Queen Yang's haunted city); River of the Dead carrying corpses; necropolis of frozen dead; **heat catastrophe** at climax; decadent, dying civilization. |
| **Burroughs** | **Drying seas** compelled migration; "dead sea bottoms"; lost archives, records, literature; "dying planet"; atmosphere plant built to sustain life; "waning resources." Collapse over "centuries" of readjustment — rapid in geological terms. |
| **Lewis** | Earth is "Silent Planet" — quarantined; Mars (Malacandra) still living. Implied cosmic catastrophe on Earth, not Mars. |

**Convergence:** **Arnold and Burroughs** both describe a **recent** civilizational collapse: ruined/dying world, lost knowledge, catastrophe within living memory of the survivors. Burroughs: rapid sea drying. Arnold: heat catastrophe, ruined cities, River of Dead.

### Systems of Compliance and Control

| Author | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Arnold** | **Yellow-robed slave caste** — "slaves, a race apart, despised by all"; porter slaves; annual tribute (Heru seized by Thither envoys); King Ar-hap demands proof tasks. |
| **Burroughs** | **River Iss pilgrimage** — all Martians taught journey to "paradise" leads to death; Holy Therns and First Born prey on pilgrims; religious fraud as control. **Layered conspiracy**: Therns think they rule; First Born rule Therns. North pole Okar: Salensus Oll, tyrannical jeddak. |
| **Lewis** | Oyarsa governs Malacandra; Bent One (Satan) has quarantined Earth. Moral hierarchy, not oppressive—but control structure present. |

**Convergence:** **Arnold and Burroughs** both describe **slave/underclass** and **tyrannical or tribute-extracting rulers**. Burroughs adds religious fraud (pilgrimage trap). Arnold: slave caste doing all manual labour; tribute exacted by Thither-folk.

### Telepathic Technology

| Author | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Arnold** | An learns language via **hypnotic mind-to-mind transfer**. No explicit telepathy elsewhere. |
| **Burroughs** | **Thoats** and **calots** controlled telepathically; **Lotharians** (Thuvia) — ancient race surviving by telepathic projections that can kill; "civilization reduced to debating philosophy, nearly extinct." |
| **Wells** | **Crystal Egg** — paired crystals enable two-way visual surveillance between Earth and Mars. "En rapport" — sympathetic link. Martians observed through crystal. |
| **Lewis** | Oyarsa as planetary intelligence; Malacandrians rational and communicative. |

**Convergence:** **Telepathy or mind-link** appears in Arnold (language transfer), Burroughs (beast control, Lotharian projections), and Wells (crystal rapport). Burroughs' Lotharians = telepathic remnant civilization — aligns with "telepathic purge" thesis in `wotw-telepathic-purge.md`.

### Plant Life

| Author | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Arnold** | Flowers, trees, shrubberies; "carpet of small red flowers"; gardens; "gigantic pink blossoms"; flowery lanes; gardens "coming into leaf"; trees budding. **Carnivorous/mobile flora** in forest: color-shifting plants, ambulatory vegetation, predatory birds eating mobile blossoms. |
| **Burroughs** | **Ochre moss** on dead sea bottoms; **mantalia** (milk-plant) — distills from soil, air, sun; **Plant Men** at south pole (Valley Dor) — vegetative beings, predatory. Valley Dor: "red grass," "park-like" cultivation. |

**Convergence:** Both describe **unusual flora** — Burroughs: moss, milk-plant, Plant Men. Arnold: fast-growing flowers, carnivorous/mobile plants. **Plant Men** (Burroughs) and **animated predatory plants** (Arnold) = vegetative beings as threat.

### Animal Life

| Author | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Arnold** | **Giant swimming stag** tows Jones to shore; **enormous rat-like creatures** in nocturnal battle; mobile/carnivorous plants; birds devouring blossoms. |
| **Burroughs** | **Thoat** — 10 ft, 8 legs, slate colour, telepathically guided; **Calot** — frog-like head, 3 rows of tusks, 10 legs, obeys telepathically; **banths** (lion-like); **white apes**; **Plant Men**. "Only one mammal on Mars"; "multiplicity of legs" characteristic. |

**Convergence:** Both use **non-human fauna** — Burroughs: systematic (multi-limbed, telepathic beasts). Arnold: giant stag, rat-creatures. **Multiplicity of limbs** (Burroughs) and **exotic creatures** (Arnold) = distinct Martian zoology.

### City Locations

| Author | Cities / Geography |
|--------|--------------------|
| **Arnold** | **Seth** — Hither city, canals, booths, ruins; **Queen Yang's** ruined city (necropolis); Thither capital (thatched); port with gourd-canoes. |
| **Burroughs** | **Helium**, **Zodanga** (Red Martian city-states); **Korad** — ancient harbor city, stranded by receded sea; **Okar** / **Kadabra** — north pole, Yellow Martians; **Bantoom** — Kaldane territory; **Gathol** — mountain city, crept down as ocean receded. |
| **Lewis** | Malacandra has handramits (canals/valleys) and habitation; no city names given in index. |

**Convergence:** **Canals** in Arnold (Seth) and Burroughs (Martian canals as "ultimate salvation"). **Harbor/port cities stranded by receded water** — Arnold: Seth decaying; Burroughs: Korad, Gathol. **Ruined or ancient cities** — both.

### Government Styles

| Author | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Arnold** | **Prince Hath** — nominal ruler of Hither; **King Ar-hap** — Thither warlord; **Government** (capitalised) — Hither bureaucracy; **annual lot marriage**; slave caste beneath free citizens. |
| **Burroughs** | **Jeddaks** (kings) of city-states; **Holy Therns** — white priestly caste, religious hierarchy; **First Born** — Black Martians, rule Therns; **Salensus Oll** — jeddak of Okar. City-state principalities (Helium, Zodanga, etc.). |
| **Lewis** | **Oyarsa** — planetary angel/intelligence governing Malacandra; three species with distinct roles. |

**Convergence:** **Monarchical** (kings, princes, jeddaks) and **hierarchical** (priests, castes). Arnold and Burroughs: **tribute, conquest, captive-taking** (Heru; pilgrims). Burroughs: multi-layered religious/political conspiracy at poles.

## III. Poles as Forbidden Zones (Burroughs Only in Collection)

Only Burroughs in this collection describes polar forbidden zones in detail:

- **South pole:** Valley Dor, River Iss, Holy Therns, Plant Men, First Born. Pilgrimage = death trap.
- **North pole:** Okar, Kadabra, Yellow Martians. Concealed kingdom. "Hothouse cities" — last habitable zone.

Arnold has **River of the Dead** (one-way, carries corpses to falls) and **ice valley necropolis** — thematically similar (death-flow, frozen dead) but not explicitly polar. **Convergence of motif:** death-river, frozen dead, hidden/forbidden regions.
