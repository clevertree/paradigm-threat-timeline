## Overview

A scholarly website arguing that world mythology preserves accurate eyewitness memories
of real catastrophic astronomical events. The site synthesises **comparative mythology**,
**archaeoastronomy**, **plasma physics**, and **catastrophism** to reconstruct a prehistoric
sky that was radically different from today's.

Core thesis: myths are not allegory — they are the oral-historical record of a sky dominated
by close planetary encounters and large-scale plasma phenomena visible to the naked eye.
The site contains three interlocking book-length works presented as sequential web pages.

## ★ Anthony Peratt & The Peratt Column

### Who is Anthony Peratt?

**Anthony L. Peratt** (Los Alamos National Laboratory) is a plasma physicist who proposed
that prehistoric rock art worldwide depicts a massive **plasma discharge column** — the
"Peratt Column" — visible in the ancient sky during a period of intense high-energy plasma
activity. His research connects:

- **Z-pinch / Bennett pinch** plasma instabilities → characteristic hourglass/humanoid shapes
- **Birkeland currents** → twisted rope-like column structures seen in rock art globally
- **Ball plasmoids** → bright spheres at the top and bottom of the discharge column
- **Worldwide rock art convergence** → over 20,000 petroglyph sites on every inhabited continent
   independently depicting the same sequence of plasma morphologies

His findings were published in:
- *IEEE Transactions on Plasma Science*, 2003 — "Characteristics for the Occurrence of a High-Current, Z-Pinch Aurora as Recorded in Antiquity" (Part 1)
- *IEEE Transactions on Plasma Science*, 2007 (Part 2, with co-authors)

## Key Themes Across the Site

- **Comparative mythology** — global parallels in creation stories, flood myths, sky gods, world ages
- **Catastrophism** — astronomical catastrophe as the engine of mythic invention
- **Saturn theory** — Saturn as primordial sun / polar column in prehistoric sky
- **Plasma cosmology** — Birkeland currents, Z-pinch instabilities, ball plasmoids
- **Rock art / petroglyphs** — worldwide images as direct records of observed sky phenomena
- **Archaeoastronomy** — astronomical alignment and astronomical dating of ancient sites
- **Oral tradition** — mechanisms by which prehistoric peoples preserved sky-event memories across millennia
- **Cultural memory** — myth as encoded historical record, not metaphor or fantasy
